# How to Submit Your First MTD Quarterly Update — August 2026 Deadline

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax went live in April 2026. If you're a sole trader or landlord with qualifying income over £50,000, your first quarterly update is now due by **7 August 2026**. This guide walks you through exactly what to submit, how to do it, and what you need to have in place before the deadline.

---

## What is an MTD quarterly update?

A quarterly update is a digital summary of your business income and expenses submitted to HMRC every three months through your MTD-compatible accounting software. It is not a tax payment — it's a reporting obligation.

HMRC uses quarterly updates to see how your business is performing throughout the year. Your actual tax liability is still calculated and paid through the Self Assessment process (now called the Final Declaration under MTD).

Each update covers a three-month trading period. HMRC records the figures and uses them alongside your end-of-year submission to calculate what you owe.

## What do I need to include in a quarterly update?

Each quarterly update is a simple summary of two things:

1. **Total business income** received during the period
2. **Total allowable expenses** incurred during the period, split into HMRC's standard categories

You do not need to provide individual transaction details or receipts in the update itself. Your software submits the summary figures, but you are expected to keep digital records of every transaction underlying those figures.

HMRC's expense categories for self-employment income:
- Cost of goods bought for resale or goods used
- Construction industry — payments to subcontractors
- Wages, salaries, and other staff costs
- Car, van, and travel expenses
- Rent, rates, power, and insurance costs
- Repairs and maintenance of property and equipment
- Accountancy, legal, and other professional fees
- Interest and bank, credit card charges
- Phone, fax, stationery, and other office costs
- Advertising and business entertainment costs
- Other business expenses

Your accounting software will map your categorised transactions to these headings and submit the totals automatically.

## How to submit your quarterly update (step by step)

The exact steps depend on your software, but the general process is the same across all HMRC-recognised platforms:

**Step 1: Make sure your transactions are up to date**

Before submitting, ensure all income and expenses for the quarter are recorded and categorised in your software. If you use bank feeds, check that all transactions have been imported and matched. Catch any uncategorised items before you run the submission.

**Step 2: Review your income and expense totals**

Most MTD software has a quarterly summary view. Review the figures — check that total income looks right relative to what you invoiced or received, and that expenses are categorised correctly.

**Step 3: Submit to HMRC through your software**

The submission process is built into your software. There is no separate HMRC portal login required. You will typically see a button labelled something like "Submit quarterly update" or "File with HMRC". Your software sends the figures directly to HMRC via their API.

**Step 4: Save your confirmation**

After submission, your software should show a confirmation screen with a submission reference. Save or screenshot this for your records.

## What if my records are not fully up to date?

You need to bring your records up to date before submitting. Quarterly updates must reflect actual income and expenses — you cannot submit estimated figures.

If you have gaps (e.g. you haven't been keeping digital records since April):

1. Gather bank statements, invoices, and receipts for the period
2. Enter or import the transactions into your software
3. Categorise them correctly
4. Run the quarterly summary and submit

This will take time. If you are significantly behind, prioritise your income figures first, then work through expenses. Your accounting software may allow you to bulk import bank transactions if you have a CSV from your bank.

---

## What happens if I miss the 7 August deadline?

HMRC has introduced a points-based penalty system for late MTD submissions:

- Each missed quarterly update adds **1 penalty point**
- When you reach 4 points, a **£200 penalty** is charged
- Further misses above the threshold trigger additional £200 penalties
- Points are cleared after a period of compliance

HMRC has signalled a soft-landing period for the first year, but the obligations still apply. Missing the August 2026 deadline without a reasonable excuse accumulates points against your record.

The safest approach is to submit something — even if your records are incomplete — rather than miss the deadline entirely. Speak to your accountant if you are in this situation.

---

## What comes after the quarterly update?

Quarterly updates are not the end of the MTD process. At the end of the tax year you will also need to submit:

1. **End of Period Statement (EOPS)** — confirms your business figures for the year and allows you to add any adjustments
2. **Final Declaration** — replaces the old Self Assessment return. Includes all income sources, personal allowances, and deductions. Tax is calculated and payment dates confirmed here.

Your accounting software should guide you through both of these when the time comes.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: When is the first MTD quarterly update due?**
The first MTD quarterly update for the 2026/27 tax year is due by 7 August 2026. It covers the period from 6 April to 5 July 2026.

**Q: What do I include in a quarterly update?**
A quarterly update is a summary of your total business income and total expenses for the quarter, split across HMRC's standard expense categories. You do not submit individual receipts or invoices — your software submits the totals automatically based on your categorised records.

**Q: Can I submit my quarterly update through the HMRC website?**
No. Quarterly updates must be submitted through HMRC-recognised accounting software. You cannot use the old Self Assessment portal or Government Gateway directly for quarterly submissions.

**Q: What happens if my records are not up to date?**
You need to bring your records current before submitting. If you are behind, import your bank transactions, categorise them, and then submit. Missing the deadline is worse than submitting late records.

**Q: Do I owe tax after each quarterly update?**
No. Quarterly updates are a reporting obligation, not a tax payment. Your tax liability is calculated at the end of the year through the Final Declaration process. HMRC may issue in-year estimates based on your quarterly figures, but no tax is due until the year-end process is complete.

**Q: What is the penalty for missing an MTD quarterly deadline?**
HMRC uses a points-based system. Each missed quarterly update adds one penalty point. At four points, a £200 penalty is charged. Further missed submissions above the threshold trigger additional £200 penalties.
