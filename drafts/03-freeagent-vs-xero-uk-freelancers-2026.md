# **FreeAgent vs Xero for UK Freelancers: Which Is Better in 2026?**

FreeAgent and Xero are the two most commonly recommended accounting tools for UK freelancers and sole traders. They're both MTD-compatible, both UK-native in their tax support, and both used by hundreds of thousands of British businesses. So which one is actually better for a one-person service business?

Short answer: FreeAgent for most sole traders and freelancers. Xero for limited companies, growing businesses, and anyone who needs strong integrations. Here's the full comparison.

## **Pricing**

**FreeAgent:**

●      £29/month (standard)

●      50% off for the first 6 months = £14.50/month to start

●      Free with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle business accounts

**Xero:**

●      Starter: £7/month (limited invoices and bills)

●      Standard: £27/month (unlimited invoices)

●      Premium: £65/month (multi-currency)

**Winner: FreeAgent** — especially if you bank with NatWest/RBS/Mettle and get it free. Even at full price, FreeAgent's single £29/month plan includes everything a sole trader needs. Xero's Starter plan is cheaper but limits you to 20 invoices/month — you'll likely need Standard at £27/month, which is comparable to FreeAgent's full price but without the free bank option.

## **Self Assessment**

This is where FreeAgent has a significant edge. FreeAgent files Self Assessment returns directly to HMRC, and the whole product is designed around helping sole traders understand and prepare their annual tax return. The tax timeline dashboard, tax estimate, and SA filing are all seamlessly connected.

Xero does not file Self Assessment directly — it focuses on company accounts and VAT. Sole traders using Xero typically export their data and either use a separate tool or an accountant to handle SA. If you're a limited company, this doesn't matter (Ltd companies file Corporation Tax, not SA). If you're a sole trader, it's a meaningful difference.

**Winner: FreeAgent** — clear advantage for sole traders.

## **Making Tax Digital (MTD)**

Both are fully MTD-compliant and HMRC-recognised for:

●      MTD for VAT

●      MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment (ITSA) — required from April 2026 for sole traders earning over £50k

Both handle quarterly submissions, digital record keeping, and the final end-of-year declaration. No meaningful difference here.

**Winner: Draw**

## **Ease of use**

FreeAgent is genuinely simpler. The navigation assumes you're a non-accountant running a service business. The dashboard shows you your tax estimate, outstanding invoices, and upcoming deadlines without requiring you to understand accounting terminology.

Xero is more powerful but more complex. The interface is designed for bookkeepers and accountants as much as for business owners. For a freelance designer or consultant, this extra complexity isn't always welcome.

**Winner: FreeAgent** — for non-accountants running a simple service business.

## **Integrations and apps**

Xero wins clearly here. It has hundreds of app integrations — payment processors, CRM systems, e-commerce platforms, project management tools. If you want your accounting software to connect to everything else you use, Xero's ecosystem is unmatched.

FreeAgent has fewer integrations, though it connects with the main ones: Stripe, PayPal, GoCardless, and all major UK banks via open banking.

**Winner: Xero** — substantially better integrations.

## **Invoice and expense features**

Both handle invoicing and expenses well. FreeAgent has a slightly more streamlined invoice creation flow for simple businesses. Xero has more customisation options and better bulk operations.

For capturing expenses on the go, Xero and QuickBooks (not reviewed here) have better mobile experiences. FreeAgent's mobile app is functional but not as polished as Xero's.

**Winner: Draw** — with Xero slightly ahead on mobile and customisation.

## **Customer support**

FreeAgent: UK-based phone, email, and live chat. Generally well-regarded, particularly by sole traders and contractors.

Xero: 24/7 online support, no direct phone line. Some users find the lack of phone support frustrating for urgent issues.

**Winner: FreeAgent** — phone support available, UK-based.

## **The verdict**

**Choose FreeAgent if:**

●      You're a sole trader or freelancer (not a limited company)

●      You bank with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle — it's free

●      You want to file your own Self Assessment without an accountant

●      You want the simplest, most UK-tailored experience

**Choose Xero if:**

●      You're running a limited company

●      You need strong third-party integrations

●      You have or plan to have employees (payroll is better in Xero)

●      You want more detailed financial reporting

●      Your accountant already uses Xero

For the majority of UK freelancers — designers, developers, writers, consultants, photographers — FreeAgent is the better-suited tool. Xero is the better option the moment your business starts to grow beyond a one-person operation.
