# Free MTD Software for UK Sole Traders 2026 — Every HMRC-Approved Option

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax is now live, and the question most sole traders are asking is: *do I actually have to pay for software?* The answer is no — but the free options vary enormously in capability. This guide covers every genuinely free or low-cost route to MTD compliance, with an honest view of what each one can and can't do.

---

## Why free MTD software exists

HMRC requires all MTD submissions to go through recognised software. Recognising that mandating software would impose costs on small businesses, HMRC approved several free tools alongside the paid options. Some are full accounting platforms with a free tier; others are stripped-back bridging tools that only handle the submission itself.

Knowing the difference matters — especially if you want to use your software for more than just the quarterly update.

## Option 1: FreeAgent — free if you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Mettle

**Cost:** Free (with eligible bank account) or £19/month + VAT
**MTD compliant:** Yes — HMRC-recognised for income tax quarterly updates and VAT
**Best for:** Sole traders who want a complete, UK-focused accounting tool at no cost

FreeAgent is the most significant free option for sole traders, but only if you qualify. NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank, and Mettle (NatWest's digital business account) all offer FreeAgent as a free benefit to business customers.

If you bank with any of these, you get the full FreeAgent product — bank feeds, invoicing, Self Assessment integration, MTD quarterly submissions, tax timeline, and more — completely free.

**What FreeAgent does:**
- Connects to your bank via Open Banking (bank feeds)
- Categorises and tracks income and expenses
- Estimates your tax liability in real time
- Submits quarterly MTD updates to HMRC directly
- Helps you complete and submit your Self Assessment (or Final Declaration under MTD)

**What FreeAgent doesn't do:**
- Advanced project profitability reporting
- Multi-currency invoicing on the sole trader plan
- Deep inventory management

For the vast majority of sole traders — consultants, freelancers, tradespeople, creative professionals — FreeAgent covers everything you need.

## Option 2: HMRC's free digital service

**Cost:** Free
**MTD compliant:** Yes
**Best for:** Sole traders with the very simplest income situation

HMRC provides its own free digital service for MTD submissions, accessible through the Government Gateway. It's basic by design — it handles the quarterly update submission itself but does not include bank feeds, invoicing, expense tracking, or any accounting functionality.

**What HMRC's tool does:**
- Accepts manually entered quarterly income and expense totals
- Submits directly to HMRC

**What it doesn't do:**
- Track individual transactions
- Connect to your bank account
- Help with invoicing, categorisation, or record-keeping
- Assist with Self Assessment or Final Declaration

If your finances are extremely simple — one income stream, very few expenses, you already track everything in a spreadsheet — HMRC's tool may technically suffice. For anyone with regular transactions or expenses to track, it creates more admin than it saves.

## Option 3: Bridging software — use your spreadsheet and add MTD compliance

**Cost:** From free to approximately £10/month
**MTD compliant:** Yes (with a digital link to your records)
**Best for:** Sole traders who already keep good spreadsheet records and don't want to change their workflow

Bridging software sits between your existing spreadsheet and HMRC's MTD system. You keep your records in Excel or Google Sheets, and the bridging tool reads from your spreadsheet and submits the quarterly totals to HMRC.

HMRC allows this — but there must be a genuine digital link throughout. You cannot manually retype figures from one system to another.

Popular bridging options:
- **TaxCalc MTD** — free tier available for basic use
- **DataDear** — connects directly to Excel/Google Sheets
- **Xbridge** — budget option aimed at spreadsheet users

Bridging software is a reasonable option if you have an established spreadsheet system you trust and do not want to migrate to full accounting software. However, it handles only the MTD submission — invoicing, tax estimation, and Self Assessment remain your responsibility separately.

## Option 4: SumUp — free MTD tier

**Cost:** Free (basic tier)
**MTD compliant:** Yes
**Best for:** Sole traders with very simple finances, especially those already using SumUp card readers

SumUp launched MTD-compatible accounting tools in 2026. The free tier covers basic income and expense tracking and MTD quarterly submissions. It integrates neatly with SumUp's payment infrastructure, which is an advantage if you already take card payments through SumUp.

For sole traders outside the SumUp payment ecosystem, the free tier is functional but limited compared to FreeAgent's breadth of features.

## Option 5: Starling Bank — MTD included with sole trader account

**Cost:** Free (with Starling Business account)
**MTD compliant:** Yes
**Best for:** Sole traders who want free digital banking plus MTD compliance in one place

Starling's sole trader business account includes built-in MTD tools as part of the account — no separate software required. You can track income and expenses within the Starling app and submit quarterly updates directly.

Starling's MTD offering is designed for simplicity rather than depth. It works well for sole traders with uncomplicated finances who want banking and MTD in one app. It does not offer the invoicing, tax timeline, or Self Assessment integration that FreeAgent provides.

> **Choosing between Starling and FreeAgent?** If you prioritise free current account banking, Starling is a strong choice. ## Suggested FAQs

**Q: Is there genuinely free MTD software for sole traders?**
Yes. FreeAgent is completely free if you bank with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle. Starling Bank includes MTD tools in its free sole trader account. HMRC also provides a basic free digital service for manual submissions. Several bridging software tools have free tiers.

**Q: What is the catch with free MTD software?**
The catches vary. FreeAgent's free access requires you to bank with an eligible NatWest group bank. HMRC's free service has very limited functionality — no bank feeds, no invoicing, no Self Assessment help. Bridging software requires you to maintain your own spreadsheet records.

**Q: Can I submit MTD quarterly updates without paying for software?**
Yes — via HMRC's free digital service (for simple situations), bridging software (if you use spreadsheets), FreeAgent (if you bank with NatWest/RBS/Mettle), Starling, or SumUp. Eligibility and functionality vary.

**Q: Is free MTD software good enough?**
It depends on your needs. For MTD compliance alone, free tools can be sufficient. But if you want bank feeds, invoicing, Self Assessment help, and tax estimates — features that save real time — a paid tool or FreeAgent via NatWest is more practical.

**Q: What is bridging software?**
Bridging software connects your existing spreadsheet to HMRC's MTD system. It reads figures from your spreadsheet and submits them to HMRC via the MTD API. It does not do your bookkeeping — you still maintain your records in your spreadsheet. HMRC requires a genuine digital link between your records and the submission.
