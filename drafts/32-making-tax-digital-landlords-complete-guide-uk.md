# **Making Tax Digital for Landlords: The Complete Guide (2026)**

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax is not just a sole trader issue. Landlords with significant rental income are included in MTD ITSA from April 2026. This guide covers everything landlords need to know: who's affected, what the new system involves, what software works, and how to prepare.

## **Which landlords are affected by MTD ITSA?**

MTD ITSA applies to landlords whose **gross UK property income** (rent received before any deductions) meets the threshold.

| Threshold | Mandatory from |
|---|---|
| Over £50,000 | April 2026 |
| Over £30,000 | April 2027 |
| Over £20,000 | April 2028 |

**The threshold is gross rent received — not profit.** A landlord with three properties generating £55,000 in annual rent, even after mortgage and maintenance costs that leave a modest profit, has qualifying income of £55,000 — fully in scope for April 2026.

If you have **both rental income and self-employment income**, both are combined for the threshold calculation. A landlord earning £35,000 from properties and £20,000 from a small business has qualifying income of £55,000 — in scope for April 2026 even though neither stream alone crosses the threshold.

**What doesn't count:** PAYE salary, pension income, savings interest, and dividends don't count towards the qualifying income threshold.

## **What MTD ITSA means for landlords**

Under the current system, landlords report rental income once a year through a Self Assessment tax return. Under MTD ITSA, this changes to a four-times-a-year system with a year-end finalisation.

### **Quarterly updates**

Four times per year, you submit a summary of rental income and expenses to HMRC through MTD-compatible software. These are not tax payments — they're updates of your business figures.

**Standard quarterly deadlines:**
- Q1 (6 April – 5 July) → submit by 5 August
- Q2 (6 July – 5 October) → submit by 5 November
- Q3 (6 October – 5 January) → submit by 5 February
- Q4 (6 January – 5 April) → submit by 5 May

Each quarterly update includes:
- Total rent received in the quarter
- Allowable property expenses (broken down by category)

### **End of Period Statement (EOPS)**

At the end of the tax year, you submit an End of Period Statement for your property income. This confirms the year's figures and is where you make adjustments and include any annual allowances (such as the property income allowance).

### **Final Declaration**

The Final Declaration replaces the old Self Assessment return. It combines all income sources — property, employment, savings, dividends — into your overall tax position for the year. The tax due is calculated here.

## **What records do landlords need to keep under MTD?**

All records must be kept **digitally** — not on paper. This is a new requirement for landlords who currently manage finances in paper files or simple spreadsheets.

**Income records:**
- Rent received from each property (date, amount, property)
- Any other property income (service charges, parking, storage)

**Expense records:**
- Mortgage interest (noting this is subject to basic rate restriction — it still needs to be recorded)
- Letting agent fees and management charges
- Repairs and maintenance (receipts required)
- Buildings and contents insurance
- Ground rent and service charges
- Utility bills (if landlord-paid)
- Accountancy fees related to property
- Travel costs related to property management (mileage)
- Advertising costs

**Not allowable and must be excluded:**
- Capital improvements (new extension, full renovation) — these are capital costs, not revenue expenses
- Personal use costs

**How long to keep records:** At least 5 years and 10 months from the relevant tax year end.

## **How the mortgage interest restriction works under MTD**

The restriction of mortgage interest relief to basic rate (introduced gradually since 2017 and fully in effect since 2020-21) continues under MTD.

Under MTD, you still record full mortgage interest paid as an expense. However, in the final declaration, the restriction is applied — only 20% basic rate relief is given on mortgage interest, rather than full income tax rate relief.

If you're a higher rate taxpayer receiving full rental income without this restriction, you'll have been familiar with the impact of the restriction. MTD doesn't change the tax calculation — it just changes the reporting frequency.

## **Software options for landlords**

Landlords need MTD-compatible software that specifically supports **property income** — not just self-employment. Key options:

### **FreeAgent**
Strong for landlords with self-employment income too. Handles both income streams well. If you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Mettle, FreeAgent is free — a compelling option for landlords who are also sole traders.

### **Xero**
Covers property income alongside self-employment. More suitable for landlords with accountants or complex portfolios.

### **QuickBooks**
Supports property income. Good invoicing features — useful if you also have self-employment income that requires regular invoicing.

### **Landlord-specific software**
Some platforms cater specifically to landlords:
- **Hammock** — MTD-compatible, designed specifically for landlords
- **Landlord Studio** — property management + accounting, MTD-compatible
- **Arthur Online** — for portfolio landlords, includes MTD-compatible accounting

For portfolio landlords managing multiple properties, specialist software often has better property-level reporting than general accounting platforms.

### **Spreadsheet + bridging software**
If you currently manage your rental accounts in a spreadsheet, you can continue using it and add bridging software for the quarterly submissions. Check that the bridging tool explicitly supports property income quarterly updates — not just self-employment.

## **Practical steps for landlords to prepare**

### **Step 1: Check your gross rental income**
Look at your most recent Self Assessment return — UK property pages — for total rent received. Add any self-employment turnover if applicable. If the combined figure is above £50,000, you need to comply from April 2026.

### **Step 2: Choose and set up software**
Choose MTD-compatible software that handles property income. Set it up before April 2026, ideally by January 2026 to allow time to get familiar with it.

### **Step 3: Convert to digital records**
Start recording all rental income and expenses digitally. If you're moving from paper files, work through your current year's records and enter them into the new system.

### **Step 4: Sign up for MTD ITSA**
Sign up through your Government Gateway account. You'll need your UTR and NI number, and your software must be set up first. HMRC recommends signing up at least a month before your first quarterly update.

### **Step 5: Understand what to submit quarterly**
Your first quarterly update (Q1, 6 April – 5 July 2026) is due 5 August 2026. Each quarter, you log into your software and submit the summary of that period's property income and expenses.

## **What landlords don't need to worry about**

- **Quarterly updates are not tax payments.** You still pay tax at the usual times (31 January and 31 July under payments on account, or 31 January for balancing payment).
- **The mortgage interest restriction doesn't change.** MTD affects reporting, not tax calculations.
- **You don't need to change how you manage tenants.** MTD only affects financial record-keeping.
- **Overseas property is not included** in MTD ITSA (different rules apply).

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: When does Making Tax Digital apply to landlords?**
MTD ITSA applies to landlords with gross UK rental income above £50,000 from April 2026. The threshold drops to £30,000 from April 2027 and £20,000 from April 2028. The threshold is based on gross rent received — before any expenses or deductions.

**Q: Does MTD for landlords affect how mortgage interest is taxed?**
No. The mortgage interest restriction (limiting relief to basic rate) was introduced separately and remains unchanged under MTD. MTD changes the reporting system — you'll submit rental income and expenses quarterly — but the tax rules themselves, including mortgage interest relief, stay the same.

**Q: How many times a year do landlords have to report to HMRC under MTD?**
Four times a year, through quarterly updates submitted via MTD-compatible software. Each update covers three months of rental income and expenses. At year end, you also complete an End of Period Statement and a Final Declaration (which replaces the Self Assessment return). Quarterly updates are not tax payments — tax is still due at the usual times.

**Q: Can I use a spreadsheet for my rental records under MTD?**
Yes, with bridging software. HMRC allows digital records kept in spreadsheets, but you cannot submit directly from a spreadsheet — you need bridging software that reads your rental figures and submits them to HMRC. Check that any bridging software you consider specifically supports property income quarterly updates.

**Q: What software do landlords need for Making Tax Digital?**
You need HMRC-recognised MTD software that supports property income reporting. General accounting platforms (FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks) handle property income alongside self-employment. Landlord-specific software (Hammock, Landlord Studio) is designed specifically for rental portfolios and may be better for landlords without other business income.
