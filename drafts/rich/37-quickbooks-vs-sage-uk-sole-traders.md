**Choose Sage** if you want a free option, need CIS, or want UK phone support. **Choose QuickBooks** if you want the best mobile app, built-in mileage tracking, or Self Assessment filing on a low-cost plan.

Both are solid. Both are MTD-ready. The decision mostly comes down to price and which specific features matter to your work.

## QuickBooks strengths

### Mileage tracking
QuickBooks has a GPS-based mileage tracker built into its mobile app. If you drive for work, this alone can save you hours of manual logging — and potentially hundreds of pounds in missed deductions.

### Sole Trader plan value
At £10/month, the QuickBooks Sole Trader plan is specifically designed for self-employed people and includes Self Assessment filing. That's a meaningful feature at a low price.

### Mobile app
QuickBooks consistently rates highly for mobile UX. You can photograph receipts, track mileage, send invoices, and reconcile transactions — all from your phone. For sole traders who are always on the move, this matters.

### Self Assessment built in
The Sole Trader plan lets you file your Self Assessment directly through QuickBooks. If you're managing your own tax affairs without an accountant, this removes one painful step.

---

## Sage strengths

### Free tier that's actually usable
Sage's free Accounting Start tier is not a crippled demo. It handles invoicing, expense tracking, and bank reconciliation. For a sole trader with straightforward finances who just needs to stay organised and MTD-compliant, it may be all you need.

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Free tier reality check">
Sage's free tier does not include Self Assessment filing or CIS. If you need either of those, you'll need to upgrade to Simple Start (£14/mo) or above. But for basic record-keeping and MTD compliance, the free tier is legitimate.
</InfoCallout>

### CIS support
If you work in construction and are registered for the Construction Industry Scheme, Sage is the clear winner. It handles CIS deductions, CIS returns, and the admin that comes with being a CIS subcontractor or contractor.

<InfoCallout type="warning" title="CIS users — this matters">
QuickBooks does not have native CIS support. If you're a sole trader working under CIS, choosing QuickBooks means workarounds or manual processes. Sage handles CIS natively on its paid plans.
</InfoCallout>

### UK phone support
Sage offers UK-based phone support on paid plans. QuickBooks support is online-only (chat and email). If you want to speak to a human when something goes wrong, Sage is the better option.

### Upgrade path to Sage 50
If your business grows — especially if you eventually employ people, manage stock, or have more complex accounting needs — Sage has a clear upgrade path to Sage 50. You won't need to switch software as your business scales.

## Decision framework

### Choose QuickBooks if you:
- drive frequently for work and want automatic mileage tracking
- want to file Self Assessment through your accounting software
- prioritise mobile app quality
- are comfortable with online-only support
- don't need CIS

### Choose Sage if you:
- want a genuinely free option to start
- work in construction and are registered for CIS
- want UK phone support
- are on a tight budget and don't need Self Assessment filing built in
- want a clear upgrade path as your business grows

## Both are MTD-ready

This is worth stating clearly: both QuickBooks and Sage are on HMRC's list of recognised software for MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment. If you're preparing for the April 2026 MTD deadline, either platform will keep you compliant for quarterly reporting.

The difference is in how you get there and what else they do along the way.
