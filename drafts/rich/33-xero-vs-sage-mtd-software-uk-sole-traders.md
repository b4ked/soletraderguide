# Xero vs Sage for UK Sole Traders: Which MTD Software Is Better in 2026?

Both Xero and Sage are HMRC-recognised for Making Tax Digital. Both have bank feeds, expense tracking, and the ability to submit quarterly updates. So how do you choose between them?

The honest answer: it depends on your situation. Xero is the better fit for some sole traders; Sage is the better fit for others. This guide cuts through the marketing and gives you a clear, practical comparison.

<InfoCallout type="info" title="Pricing disclaimer">
Pricing in this article reflects published rates as of April 2026. Both Xero and Sage run promotional offers regularly — always check the current price on their websites before committing.
</InfoCallout>

## Quick verdict

- **Choose Xero if** you work with an accountant, use a wide range of integrations, or run a growing business with complex needs
- **Choose Sage if** you want UK phone support, work in construction (CIS), are migrating from Sage 50, or want the lowest possible cost of entry

## What both products share

Before the comparison, it is worth noting what Xero and Sage have in common for MTD purposes:

- Both are **HMRC-recognised** for MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment
- Both support **quarterly digital record-keeping and submissions**
- Both offer **automated bank feeds**
- Both have **mobile apps**
- Both support **self-employed and sole trader** use cases
- Both offer **free trials**

<InfoCallout type="deadline" title="MTD ITSA goes live April 2026">
If your qualifying income (self-employment plus property income) exceeds £50,000, MTD for Income Tax is mandatory from April 2026. Income over £30,000 follows in April 2027. You need HMRC-recognised software in place before your first quarterly period begins.
</InfoCallout>

## Pricing breakdown

### Xero

Xero has three main tiers for sole traders and small businesses:

- **Ignite** — £16/month: invoicing, bank reconciliation, expense claims, MTD submissions, short-term cash flow
- **Grow** — £33/month: adds bulk reconciliation, multi-currency (limited), project tracking
- **Comprehensive** — £47/month: full feature set including payroll, analytics

Most sole traders would use Ignite. There is no free tier.

### Sage

Sage has a more accessible entry point for sole traders:

- **Sole Trader (free)** — basic invoicing (5/month), income and expense tracking, MTD quarterly submissions
- **Standard** — £14/month: unlimited invoicing, VAT returns, CIS, full reporting, bank feeds, receipt capture
- **Plus** — £28/month: multi-currency, project costing, more advanced features

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Sage free tier for MTD">
Sage's free Sole Trader plan is one of the only genuinely free options that includes MTD ITSA submissions. If your needs are basic — tracking income and expenses, submitting quarterly updates — it may be all you need.
</InfoCallout>

## Where Xero wins

### Accountant and bookkeeper collaboration

Xero was built with accountants in mind. The adviser dashboard, client collaboration tools, and practice management integrations make it the preferred platform for many UK accountancy practices. If you work with an accountant, there is a good chance they already use Xero and will find it easier to support you on it.

### App ecosystem

Xero connects to over 1,000 third-party apps — from Stripe, Shopify, and PayPal to Dext, Hubdoc, and industry-specific tools. If your business relies on multiple platforms, Xero's integration depth is hard to match.

### Reporting and cash flow tools

Xero's built-in reports are more detailed than Sage at equivalent price points. The short-term cash flow forecast (included in Ignite) is particularly useful for sole traders managing uneven income.

### Growing businesses

If you are planning to hire staff, expand into new markets, or grow beyond self-employment, Xero scales more smoothly. The platform was designed with growth in mind, and higher-tier plans are feature-rich without feeling like a different product.

---

## Where Sage wins

### UK phone support

This is a meaningful differentiator. Sage offers UK-based phone support on all paid plans. Xero does not offer phone support — you are limited to chat and email. For sole traders who prefer to speak to a person when something goes wrong, this matters.

### CIS for construction sole traders

If you work in construction and are registered for the Construction Industry Scheme, Sage Standard (£14/month) includes CIS support. Xero only includes CIS on higher-tier plans. For tradespeople, contractors, and subcontractors, this makes Sage the more cost-effective choice.

### Price

Sage is cheaper at every tier where a fair comparison exists. The free Sole Trader plan has no direct equivalent in Xero's lineup.

### Sage 50 migration

If your business has been using Sage 50 Desktop — common in established UK trade businesses — migrating to Sage Accounting (cloud) is significantly smoother than switching to a competitor. Familiarity with the interface and terminology reduces the learning curve.

### UK-first design

Sage is headquartered in the UK and has designed its product around the UK tax system. While Xero (based in New Zealand) has excellent UK support, Sage tends to implement UK-specific features — like CIS, and specific HMRC changes — faster.

## Decision framework

**Choose Xero if:**

- You work with an accountant or bookkeeper who uses Xero
- Your business uses multiple third-party tools that need to integrate with your accounting software
- You value detailed reporting and cash flow forecasting
- You are planning to grow, hire staff, or expand
- You do not need phone support

**Choose Sage if:**

- You want the lowest possible monthly cost (or free)
- You work in construction and need CIS support without paying premium plan prices
- You want UK phone support when things go wrong
- You are migrating from Sage 50 Desktop
- Your needs are straightforward — invoicing, income tracking, quarterly MTD submissions

---

## Both are solid choices for MTD compliance

Neither Xero nor Sage will let you down on the core MTD requirement. Both submit quarterly updates to HMRC, both maintain digital records that satisfy HMRC's requirements, and both are well-established in the UK market.

The choice comes down to your workflow, your budget, and your support preferences — not MTD capability.

If you are still unsure, both offer free trials. Xero gives you 30 days; Sage lets you use the Sole Trader tier indefinitely for free.
