## Quick verdict

**Choose QuickBooks** if you want the cheapest paid option, built-in mileage tracking, or Self Assessment filing at low cost. **Choose Xero** if you're working with an accountant who uses it, you want the broadest integrations, or you expect your business to grow.

Neither is wrong. They're aimed at slightly different users.

## Where QuickBooks wins

### Price
The Sole Trader plan at £10/month is genuinely the cheapest paid sole trader software from a mainstream provider. If budget is tight and you don't have an accountant on Xero, this is a meaningful advantage.

### Mileage tracking
QuickBooks has GPS-based mileage tracking built into its mobile app. Xero doesn't — you'd need a third-party integration. For sole traders who drive regularly for work (tradespeople, consultants, photographers), automatic mileage logging can save real money at tax time.

### Self Assessment filing
QuickBooks lets you file Self Assessment directly from the app on its Sole Trader plan. Xero doesn't offer this natively. If you manage your own tax affairs and want everything in one place, QuickBooks has the edge.

### Mobile app
QuickBooks consistently outscores Xero in mobile app ratings. Receipt capture, mileage tracking, invoicing, and reconciliation all work well on mobile. For sole traders who prefer to manage finances from their phone, this is worth factoring in.

## Where Xero wins

### Accountant ecosystem
<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Check what your accountant uses first">
If you have an accountant, ask which software they prefer before choosing. Xero has the largest accountant adoption in the UK. Working in the same platform as your accountant makes year-end, tax planning, and ongoing advice significantly smoother.
</InfoCallout>

Xero is the platform most UK accountants know best. If you're working with (or planning to work with) an accountant, Xero's shared access and accountant-friendly interface can save you both time.

### Integrations
Xero's app marketplace is larger — over 1,000 third-party integrations across payments, ecommerce, time-tracking, project management, and industry-specific tools. If your business relies on specialist software, Xero is more likely to connect to it cleanly.

### Growing businesses
Xero scales better. If you're a sole trader now but expect to bring on staff, take on bigger contracts, or eventually go limited, Xero's higher-tier plans are well-suited to that growth. QuickBooks is good for growing businesses too, but Xero's ecosystem is deeper.

### Invoicing and cash flow tools
Xero's invoicing features — particularly at mid and higher tiers — are strong. Automated payment reminders, quote-to-invoice workflows, and payment integrations (like Stripe) are built in. For sole traders who invoice regularly, this is useful.

## MTD readiness — both pass

Both Xero and QuickBooks are on HMRC's recognised software list for MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment. The April 2026 deadline applies to sole traders and landlords with qualifying income over £50,000. From that point, you'll need to:

- keep digital records
- submit quarterly updates to HMRC
- submit a final declaration at year end

Both platforms handle all of this. MTD compliance should not be the deciding factor between these two — both are fine.

## Decision framework

### Choose QuickBooks if you:
- want the cheapest paid option
- drive for work and want automatic mileage tracking
- want to file Self Assessment without a separate step
- prioritise mobile experience
- don't have an accountant tied to a specific platform

### Choose Xero if you:
- work with an accountant who uses Xero (or is likely to)
- rely on third-party integrations or specialist software
- want the broadest ecosystem as your business grows
- invoice frequently and want strong invoicing tools
- expect to scale beyond a simple sole trader structure
