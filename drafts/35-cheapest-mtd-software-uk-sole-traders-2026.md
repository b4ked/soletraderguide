## Free: FreeAgent via NatWest, RBS, or Mettle

**The best free option — if you qualify.**

FreeAgent is one of the most capable accounting tools for UK sole traders, and it's available completely free to customers of NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank, and Mettle. This is not a stripped-back version — it's the full product.

**What you get free:**
- Bank feeds (Open Banking connection to your account)
- Income and expense categorisation
- Invoicing
- Real-time tax estimate (tax timeline)
- MTD quarterly updates submitted directly to HMRC
- Self Assessment filing (SA100 and submission to HMRC)

**The catch:** you must bank with an eligible NatWest Group bank. If you don't, FreeAgent costs £19/month + VAT (£22.80).

**Should you switch banks for free FreeAgent?** If you're not attached to your current business bank account, Mettle is a free, app-based business account with no monthly fees and free FreeAgent included. The combination — free banking plus free accounting software with full MTD and Self Assessment — is the most cost-effective option in the market.

## Free: Starling Bank MTD tool

**Good for: Starling Business customers who want simplicity.**

Starling Bank's sole trader business account includes built-in MTD tools at no extra cost. You can track income and expenses within the Starling app and submit quarterly updates to HMRC without a separate software subscription.

**Limitations:** No invoicing, no Self Assessment help, no detailed tax estimation. Suited to sole traders with very simple finances who use Starling as their primary business bank.

## Free: HMRC's digital service

**For: The simplest possible situations only.**

HMRC provides a basic free tool for MTD submissions through the Government Gateway. You enter your quarterly income and expense totals manually and submit. There are no bank feeds, no invoicing, no expense tracking, and no Self Assessment assistance.

This is technically compliant but adds significant manual admin. For sole traders with any reasonable transaction volume, a proper tool will save far more time than the monthly cost.

**The cheapest full-featured paid MTD option.**

QuickBooks Sole Trader is specifically designed for self-employed individuals and costs £10/month (excluding VAT). A 30-day free trial is available.

## £16/month: Xero Ignite

**Best for: Sole traders who work with an accountant, or expect business to grow.**

Xero's entry-level Ignite plan covers the full MTD requirement for sole traders — bank feeds, income and expense tracking, invoicing, and quarterly HMRC submissions. At £16/month, it's slightly more expensive than QuickBooks Sole Trader but Xero has a wider accountant user base in the UK, which matters if you share access with an accountant.

**When to choose Xero over QuickBooks Sole Trader:**
- Your accountant already uses Xero
- You need stronger integrations (Xero has 1,000+ app integrations)
- You're planning to grow beyond sole trader status

**When QuickBooks is better value:**
- You're managing your own accounts without an accountant

- You want to minimise monthly costs

## What to watch for in pricing

A few things worth knowing before you commit:

**VAT:** Most software prices are quoted excluding VAT. Add 20% — so QuickBooks £10 becomes £12, FreeAgent £19 becomes £22.80.

**Introductory discounts:** All four major providers regularly run 50–90% off for the first 3–6 months. If you're price-conscious, look for a current promotion. The introductory price is not the long-term price.

**Annual billing:** Paying annually rather than monthly typically saves 10–20%. Worth doing once you've confirmed the software works for you.

Yes. FreeAgent is free for customers of NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, and Mettle. Starling Bank includes MTD tools free with its business account. HMRC also provides a basic free digital service for simple situations.

**Q: Is QuickBooks Sole Trader different from regular QuickBooks?**
Yes. QuickBooks Sole Trader (previously called QuickBooks Self Employed) is a simplified plan built specifically for self-employed individuals at £10/month. It costs less than the standard QuickBooks Simple Start plan (£25/month) and is better matched to the needs of most sole traders.

FreeAgent (free via NatWest/Mettle) does support Self Assessment. HMRC's free digital service does not. Starling and SumUp's free tiers do not. QuickBooks and Xero paid plans include Self Assessment.
