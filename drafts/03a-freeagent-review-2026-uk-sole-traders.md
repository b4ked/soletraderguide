# **FreeAgent Review 2026: Is It Worth It for UK Sole Traders?**

FreeAgent is one of the most popular accounting tools for UK sole traders and freelancers — and it has been for years. But at nearly £23/month, it's not free. This review tells you honestly what you get, what's missing, and whether it's worth the money for a one-person business.

## **What is FreeAgent?**

FreeAgent is a cloud-based accounting software built specifically for freelancers, sole traders, and small businesses in the UK. Unlike QuickBooks or Xero, which started as broader products, FreeAgent was designed from day one for the UK self-employed market. That shows in how it works.

## **Pricing**

FreeAgent costs £19/month + VAT (£22.80 including VAT) for sole traders. There's a 30-day free trial, no credit card required.

**The free option:** If you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Royal Bank of Scotland as a business customer, FreeAgent is completely free as part of your banking relationship. This is the most significant pricing consideration — if you're choosing a business bank account, Mettle (NatWest's digital account) gives you both free banking and free FreeAgent.

## **Core features**

### **Self Assessment integration**

This is FreeAgent's strongest feature for sole traders. Your bookkeeping data flows directly into your Self Assessment tax return. FreeAgent calculates your figures, populates the SA100 form, and lets you submit directly to HMRC from within the app.

For a sole trader who would otherwise pay an accountant to file their Self Assessment, this feature alone can justify the subscription cost.

### **MTD for Income Tax**

FreeAgent is HMRC-recognised software for Making Tax Digital for Income Tax. Quarterly digital updates to HMRC are handled directly from the app. If you're affected by MTD (earnings over £50,000 from April 2026, dropping to £30,000 from April 2027), FreeAgent keeps you compliant without additional tools.

### **Bank feeds**

FreeAgent connects to most UK business bank accounts via Open Banking. Transactions sync automatically and FreeAgent learns to categorise recurring payments over time. Bank reconciliation — matching invoices to payments — is straightforward.

### **Invoicing**

Clean, professional invoices with your logo. Recurring invoices, invoice reminders for late payment, and invoice tracking. It's not the most powerful invoicing tool on the market, but it covers what most sole traders need.

### **Tax timeline**

FreeAgent's tax timeline dashboard shows your estimated tax liability, payment on account dates, and what you owe HMRC at any point in the year. This is genuinely useful — it removes the January shock.

## **What's not so good**

### **Project profitability**

FreeAgent's project tracking is basic. If you need to track costs and profitability by project or client in detail, QuickBooks or Xero serve better.

### **Reporting**

The reporting section is functional but limited. Basic P&L and balance sheet are there, but deeper analysis requires exporting to a spreadsheet.

### **Learning curve for complete beginners**

FreeAgent is one of the easier accounting tools on the market, but there's still a learning curve if you've never done any bookkeeping. Expect to spend a few hours getting familiar with it.

## **Who is FreeAgent best for?**

●      UK sole traders and freelancers who file their own Self Assessment

●      Anyone who banks with NatWest, RBS, or uses Mettle (free subscription)

●      People who want to avoid paying an accountant for their annual tax return

●      Those affected by Making Tax Digital who want the simplest compliance path

## **Verdict**

FreeAgent is the best accounting software for most UK sole traders, primarily because of the Self Assessment and MTD integrations. The tax timeline feature alone makes it worth using. At £22.80/month it's not cheap, but compared to accountant fees for the same work, it pays for itself quickly.

If you're with NatWest or RBS, there's no decision to make — it's free. Start there.
