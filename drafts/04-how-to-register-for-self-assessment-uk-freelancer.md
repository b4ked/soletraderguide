# **How to Register for Self Assessment as a UK Freelancer — Step by Step (2026)**

If you've started freelancing or earning any self-employed income over £1,000 in a tax year, you're required to register for Self Assessment with HMRC. It's not complicated, but there are specific deadlines and a few things worth knowing before you start.

This guide walks through the full process, from first registration to receiving your UTR number and filing your first return.

## **Do you need to register?**

You need to register for Self Assessment if any of the following apply:

- Your self-employed income was more than £1,000 in a tax year (after the trading allowance)
- You have income from renting out property
- You earn money from savings, investments, or dividends above the tax-free allowances
- You're a company director
- You have income from abroad
- HMRC sends you a notice to complete a return

The £1,000 threshold is your gross income, not profit. If you made £1,500 selling services online, even if you spent £1,200 on costs, you're above the threshold.

## **When is the registration deadline?**

You must register by **5 October** following the end of the tax year in which you started freelancing.

The UK tax year runs from 6 April to 5 April. So:

- Started freelancing between April 2025 and April 2026 → register by 5 October 2026
- Started freelancing between April 2024 and April 2025 → should have registered by 5 October 2025

**Late registration can result in penalties.** If you're already past the deadline, register immediately — HMRC is more lenient with late registrations than with non-registrations.

## **Step 1: Create a Government Gateway account**

If you don't already have a Government Gateway account, go to gov.uk and create one. You'll need:

- Your National Insurance number
- A valid UK address
- An email address
- A form of ID (passport or driving licence)

If you already have a Government Gateway account (for example, from checking a tax code or claiming Child Benefit), use that — don't create a new one.

## **Step 2: Register as self-employed**

Once you have a Government Gateway account, go to: gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment

Select the appropriate option and follow the prompts to tell HMRC you're starting self-employment. You'll need to provide:

- The date you started trading
- Your business type (most freelancers select sole trader)
- A brief description of your business
- Your National Insurance number

The online process takes about 10 minutes.

## **Step 3: Receive your UTR number**

After registering, HMRC will send your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) to your address by post. This typically takes 7–21 days (longer if you're abroad).

Your UTR is a 10-digit number. Keep it safe — you'll need it every year to file your return. If you lose it, you can retrieve it from your HMRC online account or by contacting HMRC directly.

## **Step 4: Set up your accounting records**

Once registered, start keeping digital records of all your income and expenses. HMRC requires you to keep records for at least 5 years after the relevant tax year.

This is also the right moment to choose accounting software — particularly important if you'll be affected by Making Tax Digital for Income Tax.

## **When is your first tax return due?**

Your first Self Assessment return will cover the tax year in which you started freelancing. The filing deadlines are:

- Online: 31 January following the end of the tax year
- Paper: 31 October following the end of the tax year

Example: if you started freelancing in July 2025, your first return covers the 2025/26 tax year (April 2025 to April 2026), and is due by 31 January 2027 (online).

## **What about payment on account?**

Once you owe more than £1,000 in tax for a year, HMRC requires you to make two advance payments toward the following year's tax bill. These are called payments on account:

- First payment: 31 January (same time as your Self Assessment return)
- Second payment: 31 July

Each payment is 50% of your previous year's tax bill. This catches many freelancers off guard in their first year of trading — you may owe both your current year's tax and the first payment on account simultaneously in January.

**Tip:** set aside 25–30% of your income each month for tax. This covers Income Tax, Class 4 National Insurance, and a buffer for the payment on account.

## **What if you've been freelancing for a while without registering?**

Register immediately. HMRC can issue penalties for late registration, but the amount depends on how much tax was unpaid and whether the failure was deliberate. In most cases, registering late voluntarily with an explanation results in reduced penalties compared to HMRC discovering the omission.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: When is the deadline to register for Self Assessment?**
You must register by 5 October following the end of the tax year in which you started freelancing. For example, if you started in the 2025–26 tax year (ending 5 April 2026), you must register by 5 October 2026.

**Q: How long does it take to get a UTR number after registering?**
HMRC posts your UTR to your registered address within 7–21 days of registration. You can also find it in your HMRC online account immediately after registering.

**Q: What do I need to register for Self Assessment?**
You need a Government Gateway account, your National Insurance number, a valid UK address, and the date you started trading. The online registration takes about 10 minutes.

**Q: What happens if I miss the Self Assessment registration deadline?**
Register immediately — HMRC charges penalties for late registration, but they're usually reduced if you register voluntarily rather than waiting for HMRC to find you. The penalty is based on how much tax was outstanding.

**Q: Do I need to register for Self Assessment if I'm employed and have freelance income?**
Yes, if your self-employment income exceeds £1,000 in a tax year (the trading allowance). You must register even if you pay tax through PAYE in your day job.
