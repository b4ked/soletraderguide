# **Do I Need to Register for VAT as a Sole Trader? The £90,000 Threshold Explained**

VAT is one of the most misunderstood taxes for small businesses. Many sole traders either don't know when they need to register or worry about it when they don't need to. This guide gives you the clear facts.

## **What is the VAT threshold?**

In the 2025–26 tax year, you must register for VAT if your VAT-taxable turnover (sales) exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period. This threshold was increased from £85,000 in the Spring Budget 2024.

This is turnover, not profit. If your total sales (before any expenses) hit £90,000, you're required to register.

## **When exactly do you have to register?**

You must register within 30 days of the end of the month in which you exceeded the threshold. So if your cumulative sales over 12 months cross £90,000 on 15 March, you must register by 30 April.

The clock runs on a rolling 12 months, not on a tax year. Check your cumulative sales every month if you're approaching the threshold.

## **What happens if you don't register when you should?**

HMRC will charge you the VAT you should have collected plus interest and potentially penalties. This can be a significant amount because you'll owe VAT on sales you've already made without charging it to customers.

## **What VAT registration means in practice**

Once registered, you must:

●      Add VAT (typically 20%) to all your invoices to VAT-registered clients

●      Submit quarterly VAT returns to HMRC (mandatory under Making Tax Digital)

●      Pay HMRC the VAT you've collected minus the VAT you've paid on business expenses

The VAT you collect from clients is not your income — you're collecting it on HMRC's behalf and paying it over quarterly.

## **The cash flow challenge**

VAT creates a cash flow consideration. You collect VAT from clients each month but only pay it to HMRC quarterly. Some businesses use this as short-term cash flow. Others find the quarterly payment a shock if they haven't set the money aside.

## **Should you register voluntarily?**

Voluntary VAT registration — registering before you hit the threshold — makes sense in some situations:

●      [object Object]They can reclaim the VAT you charge, so it costs them nothing extra. You benefit from reclaiming VAT on your own purchases.

●      [object Object]Being VAT registered lets you reclaim VAT on purchases — equipment, software, etc.

●      [object Object]Some larger clients and public sector buyers prefer working with VAT-registered suppliers.

**Don't register voluntarily if:** most of your clients are individuals (consumers) who can't reclaim VAT — they'll simply pay 20% more, which could price you out of the market.

## **VAT and Making Tax Digital**

VAT returns are already within MTD — you must use MTD-compatible software to submit them. This has been the case for VAT-registered businesses since 2019. If you register for VAT, make sure your accounting software is MTD-compatible (FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks all are).
