# **Sage Accounting Review 2026: Is It Worth It for UK Sole Traders?**

Sage is one of the most established names in UK accounting software — used by millions of businesses for decades. But Sage's history is mostly with small and medium businesses, not sole traders. This review looks at whether Sage Accounting is a good fit for a one-person business in 2026.

## **What is Sage Accounting?**

Sage Accounting (formerly Sage One) is Sage's cloud-based accounting platform for small businesses. It's distinct from Sage 50 (the desktop product aimed at larger businesses) and Sage Payroll. For sole traders, Sage Accounting Start or Sage Accounting are the relevant plans.

Sage is a British company (headquartered in Newcastle) with strong UK market presence and HMRC recognition for Making Tax Digital.

## **Pricing**

Sage Accounting UK pricing (2025-26):

- **Sage Accounting Start:** £12/month + VAT (£14.40/month) — basic invoicing, expense tracking, bank reconciliation
- **Sage Accounting:** £22/month + VAT (£26.40/month) — adds cash flow forecasting, multi-currency, detailed reporting, MTD for Income Tax submissions

Most sole traders needing MTD compliance will need the full Sage Accounting plan at £26.40/month, not Start.

Sage regularly offers introductory discounts — verify the renewal price before signing up. There is no free tier.

## **MTD for Income Tax**

Sage Accounting is HMRC-recognised for Making Tax Digital for Income Tax. The quarterly submission workflow is built in. Digital record keeping requirements are met by default.

The MTD implementation in Sage is competent but not especially streamlined for sole traders. It works, but the interface assumes some accounting knowledge.

## **Self Assessment**

Like QuickBooks and Xero (but unlike FreeAgent), **Sage Accounting does not file Self Assessment tax returns directly to HMRC.**

You'll need to manage your SA filing separately — either through HMRC's own portal using your Sage data, or through an accountant. For a sole trader who manages their own tax return, this is a meaningful limitation.

## **Ease of use**

Sage has historically been seen as less intuitive than FreeAgent. The interface has improved significantly in recent years, but it still uses more accountancy terminology than a non-accountant needs. The dashboard is functional but not as immediately clear as FreeAgent's tax timeline view.

For a sole trader with no accounting background, there's a steeper learning curve than with FreeAgent.

## **Where Sage stands out**

**Reporting:** Sage's reporting suite is strong — better than FreeAgent, broadly comparable to Xero. Cash flow forecasting is included at the standard plan level.

**Brand longevity and accountant familiarity:** Sage is deeply embedded in UK accountancy. Many accountants know it well. If you use an accountant who works with Sage, collaboration is straightforward.

**Customer support:** UK-based phone and online support, generally well-regarded for issue resolution.

**Trade and construction businesses:** Sage has strong penetration in trades. If you're a sole trader builder, plumber, or electrician, Sage may be familiar from industry contacts and has features (like CIS — Construction Industry Scheme — support) that FreeAgent lacks.

## **Who is Sage Accounting best for?**

- Sole traders in **construction or trades** who need CIS support
- Those who already **work with an accountant who uses Sage**
- Businesses wanting **strong financial reporting** at the standard plan level
- Sole traders who prefer dealing with a large, established UK company

**Not ideal for:**
- Sole traders who **file their own Self Assessment** (FreeAgent is better)
- Those wanting the **simplest, most accessible** interface
- Anyone looking for a **free-with-banking option**

## **Verdict**

Sage Accounting works well for sole traders with accountants, those in trades, or businesses that need strong reporting. But it's not the best default choice for a sole trader working independently.

FreeAgent wins on simplicity and Self Assessment integration. Xero wins on integrations and accountant ecosystem. QuickBooks wins on invoicing. Sage's main advantages — brand trust, CIS support, and strong reporting — matter more to specific types of businesses than to the average freelancer or service-based sole trader.

At £26.40/month for the full plan, it's priced similarly to FreeAgent and Xero Standard. The right choice depends more on your accountant and business type than on the software itself.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: Is Sage Accounting good for sole traders?**
Sage Accounting is a solid choice for sole traders in trades (especially construction, where CIS support matters), those who work with accountants already using Sage, and those who want strong financial reporting. For most freelancers and service-based sole traders, FreeAgent is simpler and has better Self Assessment integration.

**Q: How much does Sage Accounting cost in 2026?**
Sage Accounting Start costs £12/month + VAT (£14.40/month) for basic features. The full Sage Accounting plan is £22/month + VAT (£26.40/month) — this is what most sole traders need for MTD compliance and full reporting. Introductory discounts are common; check the renewal price before signing up.

**Q: Does Sage Accounting file Self Assessment for sole traders?**
No. Sage Accounting does not submit Self Assessment returns to HMRC. You'll need to file separately through HMRC's portal or use an accountant. FreeAgent is the main accounting platform that handles SA filing directly for sole traders.

**Q: Is Sage or FreeAgent better for a UK sole trader?**
FreeAgent is better for most sole traders who file their own Self Assessment. Sage is better for those in construction (CIS support), who have accountants using Sage, or who need more detailed financial reporting. If you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Mettle, FreeAgent is free — which simplifies the decision significantly.

**Q: Is Sage MTD-compliant for sole traders?**
Yes. Sage Accounting is HMRC-recognised for Making Tax Digital for Income Tax. The full Sage Accounting plan (not Start) handles quarterly digital submissions and meets MTD record-keeping requirements.
