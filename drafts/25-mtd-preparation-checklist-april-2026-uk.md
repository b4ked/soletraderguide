# **Making Tax Digital Checklist: What Sole Traders Need to Do Before April 2026**

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax becomes mandatory for sole traders with income over £50,000 from April 2026. If that includes you, there are specific steps to complete before your first quarterly update is due. This checklist walks through everything in order.

## **Step 1: Check if MTD applies to you**

MTD for Income Tax (MTD ITSA) applies to you from April 2026 if your **gross self-employment income** (turnover, not profit) exceeded £50,000 in any tax year from 2024-25 onwards.

Key points:
- It's based on **gross income**, not profit — if your turnover was over £50,000 but profit lower, you're still in scope
- If you also have property income, gross income from both sources is combined when assessing the threshold
- Income below £50,000 is covered from April 2027 (£30,000+) and April 2028 (£20,000+)

If you're not sure whether you're in scope: check your 2024-25 Self Assessment return for your total self-employment turnover figure.

## **Step 2: Get your National Insurance number and UTR ready**

You'll need both to sign up for MTD ITSA:
- **UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference)** — the 10-digit number on your Self Assessment correspondence
- **National Insurance number** — on your payslip, P60, or HMRC letters

If you've lost either, you can retrieve your UTR through your Government Gateway account and your NI number through HMRC's online services.

## **Step 3: Choose your MTD software**

MTD requires HMRC-recognised software for quarterly submissions. You have two main routes:

**Option A: Full accounting software**
FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks, or Sage — these handle records, bank feeds, quarterly submissions, and (in FreeAgent's case) Self Assessment.

**Option B: Spreadsheet + bridging software**
Continue using Excel or Google Sheets for records; use bridging software (Absolute Bridging, DataDear, BTCSoftware) for the quarterly submission step. Cheaper but requires more manual input.

If you're not already using accounting software, choose and set up your software **before** the April 2026 start date — ideally by January 2026 to allow time to get familiar with it.

## **Step 4: Sign up for MTD ITSA through HMRC**

You must **actively sign up** for MTD ITSA — it doesn't happen automatically. Sign up through your Government Gateway account (the same account you use for Self Assessment).

Sign up is available from HMRC's MTD sign-up service. You'll need:
- Government Gateway user ID and password
- UTR and NI number (Step 2)
- Your chosen compatible software already set up

**When to sign up:** HMRC recommends signing up at least one month before your first quarterly update is due. For April 2026 starts, that means signing up by March 2026 at the latest.

**Important:** Don't sign up mid-way through a tax year unless you're ready to use MTD from that point. Once signed up, you're in the MTD system.

## **Step 5: Set up digital records**

From the start of your first MTD quarter, all business income and expenses must be kept in digital form. This means:

- Every income transaction recorded in your software (or spreadsheet)
- Every business expense recorded digitally — category, amount, date
- No paper-only records for transactions that need to be in your quarterly update

If you're moving from paper records or a manual system, this is the step that requires the most change. Use the months before April 2026 to:
- Import or re-enter any records from the current tax year into your new software
- Set up bank feeds so transactions import automatically
- Establish a regular routine for categorising expenses

## **Step 6: Understand the quarterly update schedule**

Under MTD, you submit four quarterly updates per tax year, then an End of Period Statement and Final Declaration at year end.

**Standard quarters (April start):**
- Q1: 6 April – 5 July → submit by 5 August
- Q2: 6 July – 5 October → submit by 5 November
- Q3: 6 October – 5 January → submit by 5 February
- Q4: 6 January – 5 April → submit by 5 May

**Calendar quarters (alternative):** HMRC also allows calendar-month quarters if your software supports them. Check with your software provider.

Quarterly updates are not a tax payment — they are a summary of income and expenses for that period. Tax is still calculated and paid via the Self Assessment process (or the new final declaration process under MTD).

## **Step 7: Know your obligations at year end**

At the end of the tax year, you'll need to:
1. Submit an **End of Period Statement (EOPS)** — confirms your business figures for the year
2. Complete your **Final Declaration** — replaces the old Self Assessment return under MTD; includes all income sources, allowances, and deductions

If you use an accountant, confirm with them who is responsible for each step. If you manage your own returns, check that your software supports the full year-end process including Final Declaration.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: What do I need to do before April 2026 for Making Tax Digital?**
The key steps are: confirm you're in scope (gross income over £50,000), choose MTD-compatible software, set up digital records, and sign up for MTD ITSA through your Government Gateway account. HMRC recommends signing up at least a month before your first quarterly update is due.

**Q: Do I need to sign up for MTD ITSA manually?**
Yes. MTD ITSA sign-up is not automatic — you must actively sign up through your Government Gateway account. Have your UTR, National Insurance number, and chosen compatible software ready before you sign up.

**Q: When is my first MTD quarterly update due?**
If you start MTD in April 2026, your first quarterly update covers 6 April to 5 July 2026 and must be submitted by 5 August 2026. The quarterly submission is a summary of income and expenses — not a tax payment.

**Q: Can I still use a spreadsheet under MTD?**
Yes. HMRC allows spreadsheets for digital record-keeping, but you must use bridging software to submit your quarterly updates. The bridging software connects your spreadsheet to HMRC's MTD system. Copy-pasting figures into a different tool is not permitted — there must be a digital link throughout.

**Q: What happens if I don't sign up for MTD by April 2026?**
If you're in scope and miss the sign-up deadline, you risk non-compliance penalties. HMRC has signalled a soft-landing approach for 2026, but the obligations still apply. It's safer to sign up on time. If you're uncertain about your income threshold, seek clarity from your accountant or HMRC before April.
