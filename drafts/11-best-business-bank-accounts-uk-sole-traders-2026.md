# **Best Business Bank Accounts for UK Sole Traders 2026**

Sole traders are not legally required to have a separate business bank account — unlike limited companies, you and your business are the same legal entity. But having a dedicated account makes your bookkeeping dramatically simpler and is strongly recommended.

This guide covers the best options in 2026, focusing on accounts built for one-person businesses rather than large corporates.

## **Why you should have a separate account**

●      Separates business income and expenses from personal spending

●      Makes accounting software bank feeds cleaner and more accurate

●      Easier to track cash flow and set aside tax

●      Looks more professional to clients who pay by bank transfer

●      Required for some accounting software integrations

## **Starling Bank Business**

**Cost:** Free

Starling's business account is consistently rated one of the best in the UK for sole traders and small businesses. The app is excellent, the account is free, and the accounting integrations are strong — it connects directly to FreeAgent, Xero, and QuickBooks.

**Pros:** No monthly fee, excellent app, free UK bank transfers, good customer support, strong accounting integrations.

**Cons:** No cash or cheque deposits in branches (digital only). Some features require the paid Business Pro account (£7/month).

**Best for:** Most sole traders — especially those who want a free account with good software integrations.

## **Tide**

**Cost:** Free tier available; paid plans from £9.99/month

Tide was built specifically for small businesses and sole traders. The free account covers the basics well. Tide's standout feature is its invoice creation tool — you can create, send, and chase invoices directly from the app.

**Pros:** Invoice tools built in, easy setup (often same day), integrates with accounting software.

**Cons:** Customer support can be slow on the free plan, some users report issues with account freezes.

**Best for:** Sole traders who do a lot of invoicing and want it in the same place as banking.

## **Mettle (NatWest)**

**Cost:** Free

Mettle is NatWest's digital business account for sole traders and small businesses. The standout feature is the FreeAgent integration — Mettle customers get FreeAgent free, which is otherwise £22.80/month. For anyone planning to use FreeAgent, Mettle is an extremely attractive option.

**Pros:** Free FreeAgent subscription, no monthly fee, part of NatWest Group.

**Cons:** Fewer features than Starling, less established than the major banks.

**Best for:** Sole traders who plan to use FreeAgent — the combination is free accounting software + free banking.

## **Monzo Business**

**Cost:** Free (Lite); £9/month (Pro)

Monzo Business brings the familiar Monzo experience to business banking. The free Lite account covers basic transactions. The Pro account (£9/month) adds accounting integrations, tax pots (automatic tax saving), and expense cards.

**Pros:** Excellent app, tax pots help with tax saving, well-known brand.

**Cons:** Most useful features require the paid plan.

**Best for:** Existing Monzo personal account users, or those who want tax pot automation.

## **Traditional banks**

Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds, and NatWest all offer business accounts for sole traders. They tend to have monthly fees (£5–12/month), branch access, and more comprehensive features. Unless you regularly handle cash or need in-branch services, the digital-first options above are usually better value.

## **The verdict**

For most sole traders: start with Starling (free, excellent integrations) or Mettle (free if you plan to use FreeAgent). Both can be opened in under 10 minutes from your phone.

If invoicing is your priority, add Tide to the comparison. If you want tax automation built in, look at Monzo Pro.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: Do sole traders need a business bank account in the UK?**
Sole traders are not legally required to have a separate business bank account — unlike limited companies, you and your business are the same legal entity. However, having a dedicated account makes bookkeeping significantly simpler and is strongly recommended. Mixing personal and business transactions creates headaches at tax return time.

**Q: What is the best free business bank account for UK sole traders?**
Starling Bank Business is the most consistently recommended free option — it has an excellent app, no monthly fees, and strong accounting integrations. Mettle is the best choice if you plan to use FreeAgent, as it includes a free FreeAgent subscription.

**Q: Can I use my personal bank account as a sole trader?**
Technically yes, but it's not advisable. Mixing personal and business transactions makes your bookkeeping complicated, can complicate your tax return, and looks unprofessional to clients who pay by bank transfer. Most digital business bank accounts can be opened in under 10 minutes.

**Q: Does Mettle really give FreeAgent for free?**
Yes. Mettle business account holders get a free FreeAgent subscription as part of the banking relationship. FreeAgent normally costs £22.80/month, so this is a significant saving for sole traders who want to use FreeAgent for their accounting and MTD compliance.

**Q: Is Starling or Monzo better for a sole trader?**
Starling Business is better for most sole traders — it's free with strong accounting integrations. Monzo Business is a good alternative if you want tax pot automation (automatic tax saving), but the most useful features require the £9/month Pro plan.
