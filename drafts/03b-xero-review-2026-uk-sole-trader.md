# **Xero Review 2026: Is It Worth It for a UK Sole Trader?**

Xero is one of the most-used accounting platforms in the UK — especially popular with growing businesses and those who work with accountants. But is it the right choice for a sole trader? This review gives you an honest answer.

## **What is Xero?**

Xero is a New Zealand-founded cloud accounting platform with a strong UK presence. It's used by over 4 million businesses globally and has excellent integration with UK accountants — many accounting firms use Xero as their preferred platform and can access your books directly if you use it.

## **Pricing**

Xero's UK plans for 2026:

- **Starter** — £15/month — limited invoices (20/month) and bills (5/month)

- **Standard** — £30/month — unlimited invoices and bills, bank feeds, MTD

- **Premium** — £42/month — adds multi-currency

Most sole traders need at least the Standard plan to get full functionality. At £30/month it's more expensive than FreeAgent (£19/month) for equivalent features.

Xero frequently offers introductory discounts — check the current offer before signing up.

## **Core features**

### **MTD for Income Tax**

Xero is HMRC-recognised for Making Tax Digital. Quarterly submissions are handled directly from the platform. If MTD compliance is your primary need, Xero delivers it cleanly.

### **Bank feeds**

Xero's bank feed connections are excellent — one of the strongest in the market. It connects to all major UK banks and the transaction matching algorithm is good. Reconciliation is fast once you've trained it on your regular expenses.

### **Reporting**

This is where Xero is genuinely better than FreeAgent for growing businesses. The reporting suite is comprehensive — P&L, balance sheet, cash flow forecasting, custom reports. If you need detailed financial reporting, Xero is the better tool.

### **Accountant integration**

Xero has the best accountant integration of any UK accounting platform. If you work with an accountant, there's a good chance they already use Xero and can plug directly into your books. This reduces your admin and your accountant's time (and therefore their fees).

### **App ecosystem**

Xero has a large marketplace of third-party integrations — Stripe, PayPal, Shopify, Hubspot, and hundreds more. If you use other business tools, Xero likely integrates with them.

## **What's not so good for sole traders**

### **No direct Self Assessment filing**

Unlike FreeAgent, Xero does not file your Self Assessment tax return directly to HMRC. You'll need to export figures and use HMRC's service separately, or pay an accountant. For a sole trader who wants to file their own SA, this is a meaningful limitation.

### **More complex than necessary for simple businesses**

Xero was built for small businesses broadly, not specifically for sole traders. The interface and terminology are more accounting-focused. For someone who just wants to track income and expenses and know what they owe HMRC, it can feel like overkill.

### **Price**

At £30/month on the Standard plan, Xero costs more than FreeAgent for a sole trader. The extra features justify the price for growing businesses, but not necessarily for a one-person operation with simple needs.

## **Who is Xero best for?**

●      Sole traders who work with an accountant and want seamless collaboration

●      Businesses planning to grow, hire staff, or add complexity

●      Those who need detailed financial reporting

●      Anyone with complex integrations (Stripe, Shopify, etc.)

## **Verdict**

Xero is excellent software, but it's probably not the best choice for most sole traders in isolation. The lack of direct Self Assessment filing is a real gap, and the price premium over FreeAgent is hard to justify if you're a simple one-person business.

Where Xero wins: if you work with an accountant, if you're planning to grow, or if you need the reporting depth. For those use cases, it's worth the extra cost.

For most sole traders just starting out: start with FreeAgent, consider switching to Xero if and when your business becomes more complex.

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: Is Xero good for sole traders?**
Xero works well for sole traders, but it's not optimised for them. It lacks direct Self Assessment filing (FreeAgent has this), and the Standard plan at £30/month costs more than FreeAgent for equivalent features. Xero is the better choice if you work with an accountant, plan to grow, or need advanced reporting and integrations.

**Q: How much does Xero cost for a UK sole trader in 2026?**
Most sole traders need the Standard plan at £30/month. The Starter plan (£15/month) limits you to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month, which is often too restrictive. The Premium plan (£42/month) adds multi-currency — only needed if you invoice clients in foreign currencies.

**Q: Does Xero file Self Assessment for sole traders?**
No. Xero does not submit Self Assessment returns to HMRC. You'll need to export your figures and file separately using HMRC's own service or an accountant. This is a meaningful disadvantage compared to FreeAgent, which handles Self Assessment filing directly.

**Q: Is FreeAgent or Xero better for a sole trader?**
FreeAgent is better for most sole traders — it files Self Assessment directly, is simpler to use, and can be free with NatWest/RBS/Mettle banking. Xero is better if you work with an accountant (best accountant integration in the market), plan to grow, or need detailed financial reporting.

**Q: What is the cheapest way to use Xero for a sole trader?**
The Starter plan at £15/month is the lowest price point, but it limits you to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month. If you send more invoices than that, you'll need Standard at £30/month. Xero also regularly offers introductory discounts — check the current offer before signing up.
