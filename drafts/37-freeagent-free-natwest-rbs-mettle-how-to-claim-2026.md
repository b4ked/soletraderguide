# FreeAgent Free with NatWest, RBS and Mettle — How to Claim It in 2026

FreeAgent is one of the most capable accounting tools for UK sole traders. It handles MTD quarterly submissions, Self Assessment filing, bank feeds, invoicing, and real-time tax estimates. The standard price is £19/month + VAT (£22.80). But if you bank with NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank, or Mettle, you can get the full product completely free.

This guide explains exactly who qualifies, how to claim it, and whether it's worth switching business banks to get it.

## Which banks include free FreeAgent?

FreeAgent is included free with business banking from the following banks in the NatWest Group:

| Bank | FreeAgent included |
|---|---|
| NatWest Business Banking | Yes |
| Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS) Business Banking | Yes |
| Ulster Bank Business Banking | Yes |
| Mettle (NatWest's digital business account) | Yes |

The free FreeAgent benefit applies to **business customers** — not personal accounts. You need a business current account with one of these banks for the free access to apply.

**Note:** The free FreeAgent subscription covers the standard sole trader or limited company plan. It is the same product available to paying customers — not a stripped-back version.

**Accounting and records:**

## How to claim free FreeAgent with NatWest or RBS

**If you are already a NatWest or RBS business customer:**

1. Log in to your NatWest or RBS online business banking portal
2. Look for the FreeAgent offer in the "Benefits" or "Extras" section (the exact location varies by portal version)
3. Click "Get FreeAgent" or equivalent — you'll be taken to a FreeAgent sign-up page
4. Create your FreeAgent account. Your eligibility is verified automatically via your banking relationship
5. Your account will be set up on the free plan without requiring payment details

If you can't find the FreeAgent link in your banking portal, search for "FreeAgent" in the help section of your NatWest or RBS business banking, or contact their business banking helpline.

**If you are a Mettle customer:**

1. Open the Mettle app
2. Go to the "Perks" or "Benefits" tab
3. Tap "FreeAgent" and follow the activation steps
4. Your FreeAgent account will be linked to your Mettle account and activated free

## What is Mettle?

Mettle is NatWest Group's free digital business current account, designed specifically for freelancers and sole traders. It was built as a modern, app-based alternative to traditional NatWest business banking.

**Mettle features:**
- Free business current account (no monthly fees)
- UK sort code and account number
- Instant payment notifications
- Spending categories and financial overview
- FreeAgent included free as part of the account
- Managed via app (iOS and Android)

**Mettle's limitations:**
- App-only — no branch access or relationship manager
- FSCS protected (deposits protected up to £85,000)
- No overdraft or business lending
- No cash deposits via branch

Mettle works well for sole traders and freelancers who operate digitally and don't need branch banking. The combination of a free business account and free FreeAgent is the most cost-effective banking and accounting setup available to UK sole traders.

## Is it worth switching banks to get free FreeAgent?

This depends on what you're currently paying and what you value.

**The financial case:**

If you pay £19/month + VAT for FreeAgent on a standard plan, switching to Mettle saves £22.80/month (£273.60/year). If you also currently pay for a business bank account (e.g. Barclays Business at £8/month or Lloyds Business at £8.50/month), switching to Mettle also removes that cost.

Combined saving: up to £400/year or more, just from switching bank.

**When switching makes clear sense:**
- You're currently paying for FreeAgent separately
- You're unhappy with your current bank's fees
- You operate primarily digitally (no cash handling, no regular branch visits)
- You want to simplify — one app for banking and accounting

**When you might not switch:**
- Your current bank offers credit facilities, overdraft, or business lending you rely on
- You need cash deposits via branch
- You have a long-standing relationship with your current bank that provides business value

> **Worth noting:** switching business bank accounts is less complex than switching personal accounts. Most payments (standing orders, Direct Debits) can be migrated, and many sole traders find the process takes 1–2 hours to complete.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Is the free FreeAgent with NatWest/Mettle the full product?**
Yes. There is no difference between the free version via NatWest/Mettle and the paid version. You get the complete FreeAgent product — including MTD quarterly updates, Self Assessment, bank feeds, invoicing, and the tax timeline.

**Q: What happens if I close my NatWest or Mettle account?**
If you close the eligible bank account, your free FreeAgent access ends. You would either move to a paid FreeAgent plan or export your data and switch to another tool. FreeAgent allows you to export your data in standard formats.

**Q: Does FreeAgent free via NatWest include payroll?**
No. Payroll is a paid add-on regardless of how you access FreeAgent. Most sole traders don't employ anyone, so this won't be relevant. If you do need payroll, FreeAgent's payroll add-on costs extra.

**Q: Can I use FreeAgent free if I bank personally with NatWest but don't have a NatWest business account?**
No. The free FreeAgent benefit applies to NatWest **business** customers only. A personal account with NatWest does not qualify.

**Q: Does Mettle pay interest on balances?**
Check current Mettle terms — Mettle's interest arrangements have changed over time. As of May 2026, verify directly with Mettle.
