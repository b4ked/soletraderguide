# **Sole Trader and Landlord Income: How Making Tax Digital Affects You**

If you have both self-employment income and rental income from property, Making Tax Digital applies to you differently than to a pure sole trader or a pure landlord. The combination affects the threshold calculation, reporting structure, and what software you'll need. This guide explains how MTD ITSA works when you have both income streams.

## **How MTD treats combined income**

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax (MTD ITSA) was designed with the combination of self-employment and property income in mind. HMRC recognises that many people in this position are distinct from pure sole traders or pure landlords.

**The threshold calculation:** HMRC adds your gross self-employment income and gross UK property income together. If the combined total exceeds the threshold, you must comply with MTD — even if neither income stream alone would trigger it.

| Phase | Combined qualifying income | Mandatory from |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Over £50,000 | April 2026 |
| Phase 2 | Over £30,000 | April 2027 |
| Phase 3 | Over £20,000 | April 2028 |

**Example:** A freelance designer with £32,000 gross self-employment income and £22,000 gross rental income has qualifying income of £54,000 — above the £50,000 threshold. MTD applies from April 2026, even though neither income stream alone exceeds the threshold.

## **Separate reporting for each income stream**

MTD ITSA treats your business income and property income as **separate sources**, each requiring its own quarterly updates.

If you have both, you will submit:
- **4 quarterly updates for self-employment income** — covering gross income and expenses from your trade
- **4 quarterly updates for property income** — covering gross rent received and allowable property expenses

That's up to 8 quarterly updates per year, plus the end-of-year statements.

In practice, most MTD-compatible software handles both income streams within the same account — you're not managing two separate software subscriptions. The software separates the reporting internally.

## **What each set of updates covers**

### Self-employment quarterly updates
For your business (freelance/contracting income), you report:
- Gross turnover (total income)
- Allowable business expenses by category
- Any self-employment allowances (trading allowance if applicable)

### Property quarterly updates
For your rental income, you report:
- Gross rent received
- Allowable property expenses (repairs, maintenance, letting agent fees, insurance, mortgage interest — noting the restriction rules for mortgage interest)
- Property allowance (£1,000) if applicable

**Mortgage interest:** The rules for landlords restricting mortgage interest tax relief to basic rate apply within MTD as they do now. The quarterly updates capture gross rent and expenses; your final declaration handles the mortgage interest restriction calculation.

## **End of year: the final declaration**

At the end of the tax year, you complete:
1. **End of Period Statements (EOPS):** One for self-employment, one for property. These confirm the figures for each income source.
2. **Final Declaration:** Combines all income sources (including PAYE, savings, dividends) into your overall tax calculation.

The final declaration replaces the Self Assessment tax return under MTD. For someone with both employment, self-employment, and property income, all three streams are reconciled in the final declaration.

## **Software options for combined income**

Not all MTD-compatible software handles both self-employment and property income equally. Check that any software you consider:

- Supports both a **self-employment** income stream and a **property** income stream
- Can submit separate quarterly updates for each
- Handles the end-of-period statements and final declaration for both

**Software that handles both:**
- **FreeAgent** — supports self-employment and property income streams; strong for combined filers
- **Xero** — supports both, though property accounting is less intuitive for sole traders
- **QuickBooks** — supports both income streams

**Spreadsheets with bridging software:** The combination is more complex for bridging users. You'd need to maintain separate sections of your spreadsheet for each income stream and ensure the bridging tool can submit separate updates for each. Check with your bridging provider before committing.

## **Records you need to keep for both**

**For self-employment:**
- All business income (invoices, payment records)
- All business expenses (receipts, mileage log)
- Bank records for the business account

**For property:**
- Rental income records (tenancy agreements, rent receipts)
- Property expense records (receipts for repairs, insurance, letting agent invoices)
- Mortgage statements (for interest records, even with restricted relief)
- Details of any improvements vs repairs (improvements are capital, not revenue expenses)

Both sets of records must be kept digitally under MTD.

## **Key differences from pure sole traders or pure landlords**

| Aspect | Pure sole trader | Sole trader + landlord | Pure landlord |
|---|---|---|---|
| Threshold counted | Business gross income | Both combined | Property gross income |
| Quarterly updates | 4 per year | Up to 8 per year | 4 per year |
| Software complexity | Standard | Needs dual-stream support | Standard |
| EOPS at year end | 1 | 2 | 1 |
| Mortgage interest rules | N/A | Applies to property stream | Applies |

## **Practical preparation for combined filers**

1. **Calculate your combined qualifying income** from your most recent Self Assessment return (sum of business turnover + gross rent)
2. **Check which threshold applies** — if over £50,000, you need to comply from April 2026
3. **Choose software that explicitly supports both income streams** — confirm with the provider before signing up
4. **Separate your records now** — keep property income and expense records distinct from business records if you haven't already
5. **Sign up for MTD ITSA** through your Government Gateway account (by March 2026 if in Phase 1)

## Suggested FAQs

**Q: If I'm a sole trader and a landlord, do both incomes count towards MTD?**
Yes. HMRC adds your gross self-employment income and gross UK property income together when calculating whether you meet the MTD ITSA threshold. If the combined total exceeds £50,000, you must comply from April 2026 — even if neither income stream alone is above the threshold.

**Q: How many quarterly updates do I have to submit if I have both business and rental income?**
You submit separate quarterly updates for each income stream — four for your self-employment and four for your property income. That's up to eight quarterly submissions per year. In practice, these are usually handled within the same accounting software without needing separate subscriptions.

**Q: Can I use the same software for both my business and rental income under MTD?**
Yes, most major MTD-compatible accounting platforms (FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks) support both self-employment and property income streams. Check explicitly that your chosen software supports separate quarterly updates for each stream before signing up — not all budget options handle both.

**Q: Does the mortgage interest restriction apply under MTD?**
Yes. The rules restricting landlord mortgage interest tax relief to basic rate still apply under MTD. The quarterly updates capture gross rent and expenses; the mortgage interest restriction is handled in the final declaration (which replaces the Self Assessment return under MTD), not in the quarterly updates.

**Q: When do I need to sign up for MTD if I have combined self-employment and rental income over £50,000?**
You need to sign up for MTD ITSA by April 2026 if your combined qualifying income exceeded £50,000 in the tax year assessed. HMRC recommends signing up at least a month before your first quarterly update is due. Sign up through your Government Gateway account once you have MTD-compatible software set up.
