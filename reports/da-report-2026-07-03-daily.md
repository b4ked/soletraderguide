## Step 1 — HMRC & MTD News: Digital PR Opportunities

### 1a. HMRC Tax Update 2026: Timely Payments in ITSA Consultation — CLOSES 4 AUGUST

On 23 June 2026, the Government published **"Tax Update 2026: Simplification, Modernisation and Fairness"** — a 40-measure package debated in Parliament. The consultation most relevant to SoleTraderGuide readers:

**"Timely Payments in Income Tax Self Assessment (ITSA)"**
- Consultation open: 23 June 2026
- **Consultation closes: 4 August 2026** — one day before the soft close window before Q1 deadline
- Lead officials: Helen Derbyshire and Alasdair Hawkins, HMRC

**What it proposes:** From April 2029, ITSA taxpayers who also earn through PAYE would have their forecasted self-employment or property liability collected in-year through their payroll tax code — effectively paying tax month-by-month rather than in a January lump sum. Estimated to affect **2.1 million taxpayers**. For sole traders without sufficient PAYE income, HMRC is separately exploring monthly or quarterly direct payments based on forecasts.

**Why this matters for SoleTraderGuide:** The site's `employed-and-self-employed-tax-uk.mdx` covers exactly this audience — people with both a PAYE salary and self-employment income. This is a major live consultation directly affecting that readership, and no page on the site addresses it. The consultation window closing 4 August creates a hard editorial deadline.

**Content opportunity (HIGH):** A factual explainer — "Could you start paying your self-employment tax through PAYE from 2029?" — citing the gov.uk consultation page. This is a genuine PR hook: 7 million SA taxpayers have PAYE income; 2.1 million would fall into the new rules. If published within the consultation window (before 4 August), there is a plausible digital PR pitch to AccountingWEB and Tax Rebate Services.

**Digital PR pitch:** "HMRC proposes sole traders with a day job pay their tax bill through their payslip from 2029 — here's what that means and how to respond before 4 August."
