## Recommendations

### Recommendation 1 — MEDIUM: Add one internal link to a money page in the body

The post currently has 5 internal links, all pointing to other blog posts. Checklist 4 IL3 requires at least 2 links to money pages (reviews, comparisons, tools) in each new blog post body.

**Proposed change:** In the pension contributions section (section 2), add a link to `/tools/mtd-software-chooser` or `/software/best-mtd-software-for-sole-traders` where accounting software is referenced. For example:

> "...many sole traders find accounting software that integrates with pension tracking useful for this — our [MTD software guide](/software/best-mtd-software-for-sole-traders) covers the options."

Or in the "When professional advice is worth it" closing section, link to `/comparisons` as a resource for evaluating software providers once tax planning is in order.

**Rationale:** Internal links to money pages distribute link equity to commercial pages. This is low-risk and high-value for Phase 1.

### Recommendation 2 — LOW: Add HMRC GOV.UK citation links for key facts

The post states specific HMRC figures (45p mileage rate, £60,000 annual pension allowance, 7-year pre-trading rule, marriage allowance mechanics) without linking to GOV.UK sources. Adding 2–3 inline citations strengthens E-E-A-T (EE4) and provides authoritative trust signals.

**Proposed additions:**
- "45p per mile for the first 10,000 business miles" → link to `https://www.gov.uk/expenses-if-you-work-from-home` or HMRC approved mileage page
- "annual pension allowance is £60,000" → link to `https://www.gov.uk/tax-on-your-private-pension/annual-allowance`

**Rationale:** HMRC citations on YMYL financial content directly support E-E-A-T. Google's Quality Rater Guidelines for financial advice explicitly require authoritative sourcing.

### Recommendation 3 — LOW: Add a brief professional review note

Given this post recommends specific tax strategies (pension timing, PA trap avoidance, income deferral), a `<ReviewedBy>` callout or a single sentence attributing the strategies to HMRC guidance would strengthen trust signals for YMYL content (EE2).

**Proposed change:** Add a note in the closing section: "The strategies above are based on current HMRC rules. For advice specific to your situation, consult a qualified accountant."

**Note:** A disclaimer of this type is already present in the "professional advice" closing section. This may already satisfy EE2 adequately for Phase 1 — flag for SEO Agent review only.
