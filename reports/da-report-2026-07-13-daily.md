### 1b. New: HMRC Monthly Debt Collection Powers — Sole Trader Concern, Content Gap

Ross Martin's SME Tax Update 9 July 2026 flagged a significant new development: HMRC is extending its powers to collect lower-value tax debts by **direct monthly deduction from a taxpayer's bank account**, with the removal of the current £5,000 protected balance floor. This follows HMRC's 2026 Tax Update package.

Current Direct Recovery of Debt (DRD) rules require:
- Total debt ≥ £1,000
- Face-to-face verification of debtor awareness
- Protected minimum balance of £5,000 remaining after deduction

Proposed changes would remove the minimum balance floor and allow monthly automated deductions for smaller debts, with a "test and learn" rollout underway and full rollout planned for April.

**Relevance to SoleTraderGuide:** Sole traders with Self Assessment debts could be affected. SoleTraderGuide has no content on HMRC debt recovery, DRD, or what happens if a sole trader can't pay their tax bill. Multiple GB News and SME Business Blog pieces are already indexed. The story is also directly connected to the Timely Payments consultation — HMRC is building toward automated in-year collection across multiple channels simultaneously.

**Editorial angle:** "Can HMRC take money directly from my bank account? What sole traders need to know about HMRC's new debt powers." This is anxiety-driven search content with high intent.
