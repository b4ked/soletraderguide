## Step 1 — HMRC & MTD News: Digital PR Opportunities

### 1a. MTD Sign-Up Shortfall: 528,000 Sole Traders Still Not Registered — NEW

**The biggest editorial story in the MTD cycle to date.** HMRC's own press release (2 July 2026) confirmed only 350,000+ sole traders and landlords have signed up to MTD for Income Tax — against an expected 864,000. That is fewer than 3 in 10 of all mandated taxpayers.

Coverage confirmed across multiple sources this cycle:
- **The Register**: "Fewer than 3 in 10 sign up for HMRC's Making Tax Digital" — national mainstream pick-up
- **allinaccountancy**: "528k landlords and sole traders not signed up for MTD"
- **Tax Adviser magazine**: "New figures show scale of MTD challenge"
- **ByteStart**: "864,000 sole traders and landlords face new MTD reporting rules from April 2026"
- **AccountingWEB**: "MTD for IT — not going so well?" (community thread)

This is a defining moment for the MTD editorial cycle. The question 528,000 mandated taxpayers are now asking — "I haven't signed up for MTD yet, what do I do?" — is a high-intent query with no dedicated SoleTraderGuide answer. Key facts confirmed:

- Registration is still possible after April. GOV.UK guidance says: "If you did not sign up before April 6, you should sign up as soon as possible."
- No penalty points apply for late submissions in 2026/27 (soft landing) — but the obligation to comply still exists
- 111,000 of the 216,000 unrepresented taxpayers (those without an agent) have still not registered — more than half
- HMRC's guidance says it is the taxpayer's responsibility to check eligibility even if no notification letter was received

**Content opportunity — immediate:** *"Missed the MTD Deadline? 528,000 Sole Traders Haven't Signed Up — Here's What to Do"*

This angle:
- Directly serves the single largest unaddressed audience in SoleTraderGuide's topic cluster
- Has no direct competitor on the site
- Is linkable from accountancy firm resource pages (everyone is telling clients to sign up now)
- Has digital PR traction from The Register and Tax Adviser coverage — a SoleTraderGuide "late sign-up guide" would be the definitive independent resource for unrepresented taxpayers
- Natural home for the eligibility checker CTA

**Assessment: HIGH PRIORITY — editorial window is now, not next week.** 23 days to the 7 August deadline. Unrepresented taxpayers searching for late sign-up guidance will remain a significant audience through August. This is the most time-sensitive new content opportunity of the quarter.
