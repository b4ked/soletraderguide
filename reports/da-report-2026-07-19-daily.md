## Step 1 — HMRC & MTD News: Digital PR Opportunities

### 1a. Agent Update Issue 145 Published 17 July 2026 — NEW

HMRC published Issue 145 of the regular Agent Update on **17 July 2026**. This is the numbered edition that replaced the placeholder held since June. The update is organised across four sections: Tax, Making Tax Digital, HMRC Agent Services, and Agent Engagement.

Key topics confirmed in Issue 145:
- **Mandatory payrolling of benefits in kind** — phased launch announced
- **Capital Gains Tax — carried interest** updates
- **Voluntary National Insurance contributions** for periods abroad
- **Vaping Products Duty and Vaping Duty Stamps** — apply or amend approvals by **31 July 2026** (a deadline that coincides with the POA deadline)
- **HMRC Advance Tax Certainty Service** for major investment projects — new for high-value clients

The MTD section of Issue 145 is confirmed present. Full content detail at GOV.UK; SPA.org.uk published a summary on the same day. The issue does not appear to introduce new policy changes to MTD — it is primarily a deadline and process reminder in the context of 7 August.

**Editorial note:** Agent Update Issue 145 is the correct issue number to reference in any content from today. All prior DA reports flagging Issue 145 as "unpublished" are now superseded.

### 1b. Final MTD Registration Deadline: 7 August 2026 — Confirmed New Finding

Multiple independent sources published since 17 July (Vale & West, Isaacs & Co, allinaccountancy, Rosemary Bookkeeping) now confirm that **7 August 2026 is the final registration deadline for MTD Phase 1**, not an informal catch-up window. Registering after 7 August will put taxpayers in formal breach. 

Sources characterise this as: "The final registration deadline for phase one of MTD for Income Tax is Friday 7 August, the deadline day for filing the first quarterly update under the new mandatory rules."

This changes the risk profile of the late sign-up content opportunity:
- Pre-17 July framing: "You can still catch up — here's how"

The eligibility checker and software chooser CTAs become critical for an audience who must both register and submit in the same 19-day window.

### 1d. DRD — "Tackling Lower Value Tax Debts" Consultation Active (closes 28 August)

ICAEW published "HMRC targets lower value tax debt" in July 2026. GOV.UK and Ross Martin have confirmed coverage of the consultation, which extends Direct Recovery of Debts (DRD) to lower-value debts up to £10,000 via direct monthly bank deductions. A Pre-Deduction Notice (PDN) process is proposed as a safeguard.

This is a confirmed new content opportunity for SoleTraderGuide's audience of sole traders and landlords who may have outstanding tax debt. The 40-day window (closes 28 August) gives adequate time to commission a post. The "Can HMRC Take Money Directly From My Bank Account?" angle directly addresses fear-of-enforcement queries common in this audience.
