### 1b. ATT Publishes Practical Agent Guidance on MTD Sign-Up Errors — New Source

The Association of Taxation Technicians (ATT) published "MTD sign-up issues: practical guidance for agents" (`att.org.uk/technical/news/mtd-sign-issues-practical-guidance-agents`) covering four common failure modes in the current sign-up period:

1. **ASA login problems** — agents using the wrong Government Gateway credentials (ASA has separate credentials from older agent online services accounts)
2. **Client not found** — caused by authorisations not copied to the Agent Services Account
3. **Client ineligibility errors** — some clients are in automatic exemption or 2026/27 deferral categories; the error message reflects HMRC eligibility logic, not data errors
4. **NINO discrepancies** — incorrect NI numbers causing match failures, requiring HMRC correction

**New editorial angle:** This piece confirms that sign-up errors are widespread enough to warrant a dedicated ATT guidance document. SoleTraderGuide has no content addressing "what to do if your accountant can't sign you up for MTD" or "common MTD sign-up error messages explained." This is a practical, high-intent search topic that accountants and their clients will be searching in the next 26 days. A short troubleshooting post would fill the gap.

**Cross-reference:** The ATT exemption content also confirms that some clients flagged as "ineligible" in the sign-up flow are correctly exempt — not an error. This nuance should appear in the eligibility checker tool or a supporting post.

### 1c. SA109 / Averaging Relief Exemption — New Exemption Nuance, Potential Content Gap

HMRC updated its MTD exemption guidance (confirmed via ICAEW, ATT, CIOT) to address SA109 supplementary page taxpayers. The key changes:

- **Auto-exempt**: Taxpayers who included SA109 in their 2024/25 SA return are automatically exempt from MTD for 2026/27
- **Application route**: Taxpayers who did not include SA109 in 2024/25 but believe they will include it in 2025/26 or 2026/27 can apply for exemption
- **Averaging relief**: Partners who claimed averaging relief via SA104 in 2024/25 are also automatically exempt for 2026/27

CIOT confirmed: "HMRC confirm all SA109 taxpayers exempt from Making Tax Digital until April 2027."

**Relevance to SoleTraderGuide:** The MTD eligibility checker at `/tools/mtd-eligibility-checker` should surface these exemption categories. If it does not ask about SA109 completion or averaging relief, it may be telling some users they must comply when they are in fact deferred. This is a tool accuracy issue. Worth flagging to the SEO/Write-Up Agent for review.

SoleTraderGuide has no standalone post covering MTD exemptions. The site's qualifying income post (`what-is-qualifying-income-mtd-itsa-uk.mdx`) addresses who is in scope but not who is exempt. Search queries for "am I exempt from MTD" and "MTD exemption list 2026" have no SoleTraderGuide result.

### 1e. Timely Payments in ITSA — 9 Days to Editorial Deadline

The HMRC Timely Payments in Income Tax Self Assessment consultation (`gov.uk/government/consultations/timely-payments-in-income-tax-self-assessment`) runs to 4 August 2026. Responses via `timelypayment@hmrc.gov.uk`.

Key facts confirmed via Ross Martin (SME Tax Update 2 July 2026) and ICAEW:
