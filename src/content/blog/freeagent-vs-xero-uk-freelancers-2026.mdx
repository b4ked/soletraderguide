FreeAgent and Xero are the two most commonly recommended accounting tools for UK freelancers and sole traders. Both are MTD-compatible, both are HMRC-recognised, and both are widely used across British small businesses. So which one is actually better for a one-person service business?

The short answer: FreeAgent for most sole traders and freelancers. Xero for limited companies, growing businesses, and anyone who needs strong third-party integrations. Here is the full comparison.

## Pricing

**FreeAgent** starts from £19/month for sole traders who sign up directly. If you hold a business bank account with NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank, or Mettle, FreeAgent is free — no strings attached. [See FreeAgent pricing and start your free trial →](/reviews/freeagent)

**Xero** starts from £16/month (Ignite plan). There is no free plan, though a 30-day free trial is available. [See Xero pricing and start your trial →](/reviews/xero)

For most sole traders, FreeAgent wins on price — particularly if you already bank with one of the qualifying providers. Even at the direct price, FreeAgent's single plan includes everything a sole trader needs, without feature gating. Xero's entry plan is broadly comparable in cost, but lacks the free banking option.

## Self Assessment

This is where FreeAgent has its clearest edge. FreeAgent files Self Assessment returns directly to HMRC, and the entire product is designed around helping sole traders understand and prepare their annual tax return. The tax timeline dashboard, real-time tax estimate, and SA filing are all joined up within a single workflow.

Xero does not file Self Assessment directly. It focuses on business accounts and VAT. Sole traders using Xero typically export their data and either use a separate tool or rely on an accountant to handle SA. If you run a limited company, this distinction does not matter — limited companies file Corporation Tax, not Self Assessment. If you are a sole trader managing your own tax, it is a meaningful difference.

## Making Tax Digital (MTD)

Both are fully MTD-compliant and HMRC-recognised for:

- MTD for VAT
- MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment (ITSA) — required from April 2026 for sole traders earning over £50,000 in qualifying income

Both handle quarterly digital submissions, digital record keeping, and the final end-of-year declaration. If MTD compliance alone is your primary concern, there is no meaningful difference between them. Not sure if MTD applies to you yet? ## Ease of Use

FreeAgent is genuinely simpler for non-accountants. The interface assumes you are running a one-person service business, not managing a multi-department organisation. The dashboard shows your tax estimate, outstanding invoices, and upcoming deadlines without requiring any understanding of double-entry bookkeeping.

Xero is more powerful but more complex. The interface is designed as much for bookkeepers and accountants as for business owners. For a freelance designer or consultant, this additional complexity is rarely welcome.

For anyone new to accounting software — or who simply wants to get on with their work rather than learn a system — FreeAgent is the easier starting point.

## Integrations and Apps

Xero wins clearly here. It has hundreds of app integrations covering payment processors, CRM systems, e-commerce platforms, and project management tools. If your accounting software needs to connect to everything else you use, Xero's ecosystem is difficult to match.

FreeAgent has fewer integrations, though it covers the main ones for UK sole traders: Stripe, PayPal, GoCardless, and all major UK banks via open banking.

For a straightforward freelance business, FreeAgent's integration range is usually sufficient. For anyone running a more complex operation — with multiple tools, payment methods, or sales channels — Xero has a clear advantage.

## Invoicing and Expenses

Both handle invoicing and expense tracking well. FreeAgent has a streamlined invoice flow suited to simple service businesses. Xero offers more customisation and better bulk operations.

For capturing expenses on the go, Xero's mobile app is more polished. FreeAgent's mobile app is functional but not as capable. If a significant part of your workflow involves photographing receipts or logging expenses away from a desk, Xero handles this better.

## Customer Support

FreeAgent offers UK-based phone, email, and live chat support. Customer satisfaction among sole traders and contractors is generally high.

Xero offers 24/7 online support but no direct phone line. Some users find the absence of phone support frustrating when an issue needs urgent resolution.

For sole traders who prefer to speak to someone directly when something goes wrong, FreeAgent's support offering is stronger.

## The Verdict

**Choose FreeAgent if:**

- You are a sole trader or freelancer (not a limited company)
- You bank with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle — it is free
- You want to file your own Self Assessment without an accountant
- You want the simplest, most UK-tailored accounting experience

**Choose Xero if:**

- You are running or planning to run a limited company
- You need strong third-party integrations
- You have or plan to have employees
- You want more detailed financial reporting
- Your accountant already uses Xero

For the majority of UK freelancers — designers, developers, writers, consultants, photographers — FreeAgent is the better-suited tool.
