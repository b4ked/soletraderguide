QuickBooks is one of the most widely-used accounting platforms in the UK — and among the most heavily marketed. But being popular does not mean it is the best fit for every sole trader. This review looks at what QuickBooks actually delivers, where it stands out, and where it falls short for a one-person business.

## What is QuickBooks?

QuickBooks is cloud accounting software made by Intuit, a US company with a strong UK presence. It covers invoicing, expense tracking, bank feeds, MTD submissions, and basic reporting. It positions itself as suitable for small businesses of all types — but that broad appeal means it is not specifically optimised for sole traders in the way FreeAgent is.

## Pricing

QuickBooks UK pricing for 2026:

- **Self-Employed** — £8/month + VAT (£9.60/month) — basic expense tracking and MTD for the simplest businesses
- **Simple Start** — £14/month + VAT (£16.80/month) — invoicing, bank feeds, MTD, basic reporting
- **Essentials** — £28/month + VAT — adds multi-currency and bill management
- **Plus** — £38/month + VAT — adds project tracking and stock management

Most sole traders need Simple Start. The Self-Employed plan is cheaper but limits you to basic income and expense tracking with no invoicing. QuickBooks frequently runs 50% off introductory offers — always verify the full renewal price before committing.

There is no free plan and no free-with-banking option equivalent to FreeAgent's NatWest and RBS relationship.

## Making Tax Digital

QuickBooks is fully HMRC-recognised for [Making Tax Digital for Income Tax](/mtd-for-sole-traders/what-is-mtd-income-tax). Quarterly submissions are built into the workflow — as you categorise transactions, QuickBooks compiles the quarterly figures in the background. Both Simple Start and Self-Employed plans are compliant.

<InfoCallout type="deadline" title="MTD for Income Tax: April 2026">
  Sole traders and landlords with qualifying income over £50,000 must comply from April 2026. The threshold drops to £30,000 from April 2027. QuickBooks handles quarterly submissions once your bank feed is connected and transactions are categorised.
</InfoCallout>

The MTD workflow in QuickBooks is functional but more complex than FreeAgent's. There are more screens to navigate and more accounting terminology throughout. For a sole trader who just wants to click submit each quarter, this can feel like unnecessary friction. That said, it works correctly and reliably.

## The Self Assessment gap

This is the most important limitation for sole traders to understand. **QuickBooks does not submit your Self Assessment tax return to HMRC.**

FreeAgent does. QuickBooks does not.

If you manage your own Self Assessment return, you will need to export your profit and loss figures from QuickBooks and file separately — either through HMRC's portal or with an accountant. This adds time and complexity at year end.

If you already use an accountant who handles your Self Assessment, this limitation matters much less. But for sole traders trying to avoid accountant fees, it is a meaningful gap compared to FreeAgent.

## Where QuickBooks genuinely stands out

### Invoicing

QuickBooks has stronger invoicing features than FreeAgent. Recurring invoices with automatic payment reminders, time tracking linked directly to invoice creation, project-based billing with different rates per client or project — these are well-implemented and valuable for sole traders who bill by the hour or manage complex client work.

If you send a lot of invoices, track time, or bill multiple rates, QuickBooks handles this more powerfully than FreeAgent.

### Mobile app

The QuickBooks mobile app is among the best in this category. Receipt capture is polished — photograph a receipt and the app extracts the relevant data automatically. Mileage tracking uses your phone's GPS to log business journeys automatically. If a significant part of your work involves capturing expenses and logging mileage on the go, QuickBooks' mobile experience is notably stronger than FreeAgent's.

### Tax estimate dashboard

QuickBooks produces a running estimate of your tax liability based on categorised transactions — visible on your dashboard throughout the year. This is a genuinely useful feature for avoiding year-end surprises, and it is well-implemented.

## Who is QuickBooks best for?

QuickBooks suits:

- Sole traders and freelancers who invoice frequently and need strong invoicing features
- Businesses that track time and bill by the hour
- Those who drive regularly for work and want automatic mileage tracking
- Anyone who already uses an accountant (who will handle Self Assessment)
- Businesses planning to take on staff or grow beyond sole trader structure

It is a weaker fit for:

- Sole traders who file their own Self Assessment without an accountant (FreeAgent is better)
- Those who want the simplest possible day-to-day experience
- NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle customers — FreeAgent is free for them
- Anyone on a tight budget who only needs basic MTD compliance

## Verdict

QuickBooks is solid, well-supported software with particularly strong invoicing and mobile features. At £16.80/month for Simple Start, it is priced competitively — though FreeAgent at £22.80/month (or free with qualifying bank accounts) includes Self Assessment filing, which QuickBooks does not.

The right choice comes down to how you work. If you do a lot of invoicing, track billable time, or use an accountant who handles your annual return, QuickBooks earns its place. If you manage your own Self Assessment and want the simplest possible MTD compliance path, FreeAgent is the better starting point.
