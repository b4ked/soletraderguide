AI tools have shifted from novelty to genuinely useful in the space of about two years. For UK freelancers — especially those handling alone the admin that a larger business would spread across a team — the right tools can meaningfully reduce time spent on work that doesn't directly earn money. This isn't an exhaustive list of every AI product available. It's a practical guide to the tools worth considering, based on what they actually do, what they cost in GBP, and whether they justify the expense.

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Start free, then decide">
  All the tools on this list have either a free tier or a free trial. Spend a week with the free version before committing to any subscription — most will tell you quickly whether they fit your workflow.
</InfoCallout>

## The Short List

Before going into detail, here is a quick reference for the tools covered below:

| Tool | Best for | Free tier? | Paid from |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Claude** | Writing, editing, proposals | Yes | £20/month |
| **ChatGPT** | Research, code, ideas | Yes | £8/month |
| **Notion AI** | Notion users only | No | £8/month add-on |
| **Surfer SEO** | SEO content writers | No | £79/month |
| **Frase** | Affordable SEO research | No | £12/month |
| **Otter.ai** | Meeting transcription | Yes | £10/month |

*Prices as of March 2026 — verify current pricing on each tool's website.*

## Claude — Best for Writing and Editing

**Cost:** Free tier available. Claude Pro: £20/month.

Claude (built by Anthropic) has become the preferred AI writing tool for freelancers who produce a lot of written work. It handles long-form content in a way that reads naturally, and it is particularly strong at:

- Drafting long-form articles, guides, and reports without the robotic cadence that affects some AI-written text
- Editing and rewriting existing copy while preserving your voice and tone
- Drafting client-facing emails — especially difficult ones involving scope creep, late invoices, or declining work
- Summarising long documents and contracts in plain English
- Writing project proposals from a brief

One practical advantage for UK freelancers specifically: Claude handles British English register well. It understands the difference between a formal "Kind regards" sign-off and a chatty "Cheers", and doesn't default to American phrasing the way tools trained heavily on US content sometimes do.

**Free tier limitation:** the free tier has usage limits that regular work users will likely hit within a few days of heavy use. The £20/month Pro plan removes these caps and provides access to more capable models.

## ChatGPT — Best All-Around Assistant

**Cost:** Free tier available. Go: £8/month. Plus: £20/month.

ChatGPT remains the most widely-used AI tool and remains the best all-around option for a wide range of tasks. For freelancers, the most useful applications are:

- **Research:** summarise a topic, gather background reading, get up to speed on a new industry before a client call
- **Code:** generate scripts, debug errors, explain code inherited from a previous developer
- **Ideas:** brainstorm project angles, content topics, service offering names, or pricing structures
- **Templates:** build reusable structures for statements of work, client briefs, or onboarding documents

ChatGPT's memory feature — which retains your preferences and working style across conversations — makes the paid tiers considerably more useful than the free tier for regular work use. The £8/month Go plan is a reasonable entry point if you want persistent memory without paying for the full Plus tier.

## Notion AI — Best if You Already Use Notion

**Cost:** £8/month add-on to any Notion plan.

If you already use Notion to manage your client work, projects, or notes, the Notion AI add-on is a natural extension. It works directly inside your workspace, so you can:

- Draft content directly inside a Notion page without switching tools
- Summarise long meeting notes into bullet points
- Generate action items from an unstructured brain dump
- Improve or reformat text you have already written

The value is the integration — there is no context-switching between applications. For a freelancer whose entire workflow lives in Notion, this is worth the add-on cost.

**Important caveat:** if you do not already use Notion, there is no reason to adopt it just for the AI features. Claude or ChatGPT are better standalone writing tools.

## Surfer SEO — Best for Content Freelancers

**Cost:** From £79/month.

Surfer SEO is a content optimisation tool that analyses what your article needs to include in order to rank well on Google — target word count, specific keywords and phrases, questions to answer, and topic entities to cover. It is not magic, but it is a genuine time-saver for freelancers who produce SEO content professionally.

At £79/month it is expensive, and it only makes commercial sense if SEO content is a core part of what you do. For a writer producing 10 or more SEO articles per month for clients, it pays for itself reasonably quickly. For a graphic designer who occasionally writes a blog post, it does not.

## Frase — More Affordable SEO Content Research

**Cost:** Solo plan from £12/month.

Frase performs a similar function to Surfer SEO — researching what your content needs to rank, and helping you structure it against a framework — at a significantly lower price point. The research feature aggregates the top-ranking results for a search query and shows you exactly what topics and questions they cover.

For freelance writers and content marketers who want SEO content assistance without Surfer's price tag, Frase is the more practical starting point. The two tools are not identical — Surfer is more sophisticated for competitive analysis — but for most freelancers, Frase covers the core need at a fraction of the cost.

## Otter.ai — Best for Meeting Transcription

**Cost:** Free (up to 600 minutes/month). Pro: £10/month.

If you run client calls regularly, Otter.ai records and transcribes them automatically and generates a summary of key points. For freelancers, the most valuable use cases are:

- **Briefing calls:** reviewing a transcript after the call rather than trying to take notes while listening
- **Scope confirmation:** having a written record of what was agreed verbally
- **Action items:** automatically surfacing follow-up tasks from the conversation

The free tier gives 600 minutes of transcription per month, which is sufficient for most solo freelancers. The Pro tier adds unlimited transcription and higher-quality summaries.

## Tools We Do Not Recommend

**Jasper** starts at £39/month and positions itself as a marketing-focused writing platform. For most freelancers, Claude or ChatGPT do the same job at lower cost or for free. Jasper makes more sense for marketing teams that need brand voice templates and collaborative workflows — not for a solo freelancer.

**Copy.ai** has a similar story. The free tier is genuinely useful, but the paid plans are difficult to justify when Claude is available at the same price point with broader capabilities.

**App Store "AI tools" apps:** most are thin wrappers around the ChatGPT API, charging a subscription to access something available directly through ChatGPT's own free or Plus tier. Check what an app actually does before subscribing — in many cases, you are paying for a slightly different interface on top of the same underlying model.

## The Practical Minimum: Starting at £0/Month

If you want to begin using AI tools without spending money, start with:

- **Claude (free)** — drafting emails, editing copy, understanding contracts
- **ChatGPT (free)** — research, brainstorming, templates
- **Otter.ai (free)** — recording and transcribing client calls

That is £0 per month and will cover the majority of what most freelancers need from AI tools. Move to paid tiers only when you regularly hit usage limits, or when a specific paid feature is clearly worth the monthly cost to your workflow.
