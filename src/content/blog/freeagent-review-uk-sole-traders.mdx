## What is FreeAgent?

FreeAgent is cloud-based accounting software built specifically for freelancers, sole traders, and small businesses in the UK. Unlike Xero or QuickBooks, which started as broader international products, FreeAgent was designed from the ground up for the UK self-employed market. That heritage shows in how naturally it handles Self Assessment, quarterly tax estimates, and HMRC submissions.

## Pricing

FreeAgent costs **£19/month + VAT** (£22.80 including VAT) for sole traders who sign up directly. A 30-day free trial is available with no credit card required.

**The free option:** If you hold a business current account with NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank, or Mettle (NatWest's digital-first account), FreeAgent is completely free. This is the most significant pricing consideration for anyone choosing a business bank account. Mettle in particular offers free banking alongside free FreeAgent — a strong combination for sole traders who want to keep costs low.

At £22.80/month, FreeAgent is not the cheapest accounting tool on the market, but compared to the cost of hiring an accountant to handle Self Assessment and quarterly bookkeeping, it pays for itself quickly for most sole traders.

## Core features

### Self Assessment filing

This is FreeAgent's clearest advantage for UK sole traders. Your bookkeeping flows directly into your Self Assessment return. FreeAgent calculates your figures, populates the SA100 form, and lets you submit directly to HMRC from within the app — no exporting, no separate software, no accountant needed for the annual return.

For a sole trader who would otherwise pay an accountant £150–£400 to file their Self Assessment, this feature alone can justify the subscription within the first year.

### Making Tax Digital for Income Tax

FreeAgent is HMRC-recognised software for [Making Tax Digital for Income Tax](/mtd-for-sole-traders/what-is-mtd-income-tax). Quarterly digital updates to HMRC are handled directly from the app — no bridging tools required. The process is largely automated once your bank feed is connected and your transactions are categorised.

<InfoCallout type="deadline" title="MTD for Income Tax: April 2026">
  Sole traders and landlords with qualifying income over £50,000 must comply from April 2026. The threshold drops to £30,000 from April 2027. FreeAgent handles quarterly submissions automatically once your accounts are connected.
</InfoCallout>

Both FreeAgent and Xero are fully MTD-compliant. If you want to compare them in detail, see our [FreeAgent vs Xero comparison](/blog/freeagent-vs-xero-uk-freelancers-2026).

### Tax timeline dashboard

FreeAgent's tax timeline is one of its most practical features. The dashboard shows your estimated tax liability updated in real time, upcoming payment on account dates, and what you owe HMRC at any point in the year. It removes the January self-assessment shock — you can see throughout the year roughly what you'll owe and set aside accordingly.

### Bank feeds

FreeAgent connects to most UK business bank accounts via Open Banking. Transactions sync automatically and FreeAgent learns to categorise recurring payments over time. Bank reconciliation — matching invoices to incoming payments — is straightforward.

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Free with qualifying bank accounts">
  Mettle (NatWest's digital business account) gives you both free business banking and free FreeAgent. If you are looking for a business account as a sole trader, this combination is worth considering before paying for FreeAgent separately.
</InfoCallout>

### Invoicing

FreeAgent produces clean, professional invoices with your logo and business details. Recurring invoices, automatic late-payment reminders, and invoice tracking are included. It covers what most sole traders need, though it lacks the bulk invoice operations and deeper customisation options available in Xero.

### Expenses and receipts

Expenses can be logged via the web app or mobile app. The mobile app allows you to photograph receipts on the go, though the receipt capture experience is not quite as polished as Xero's. For most sole traders, it is entirely sufficient.

## What FreeAgent doesn't do as well

### Project profitability

FreeAgent includes basic project tracking, but if you need to analyse costs and profitability by project, client, or job in any real detail, QuickBooks or Xero serve better. For a typical service-based freelancer this rarely matters, but it can be limiting for sole traders managing complex client work.

### Reporting depth

The reporting section covers the essentials — profit and loss, balance sheet, income and expenditure. Deeper financial analysis and custom report building require exporting to a spreadsheet. If you want rich management reporting built into your accounting software, Xero has a clear edge here.

### Third-party integrations

FreeAgent integrates with the major UK payment providers (Stripe, PayPal, GoCardless) and all main banks via Open Banking. However, it has far fewer third-party app integrations than Xero. For a straightforward freelance business, FreeAgent's integration range is usually sufficient. For anyone running a more complex operation — with CRM tools, project management software, or multiple sales channels — Xero's ecosystem offers more options.

### Mobile app

FreeAgent's mobile app is functional but not as capable as the desktop version or Xero's app. If a significant part of your working life involves managing expenses or invoices from a phone, it is worth testing on a free trial before committing.

## Who is FreeAgent best for?

FreeAgent is the strongest fit for:

- UK sole traders and freelancers who manage their own Self Assessment
- Anyone who banks with NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, or Mettle — it is free
- People who want to avoid paying an accountant for their annual tax return
- Sole traders affected by MTD for Income Tax who want the simplest compliance path
- Freelancers who want purpose-built UK software rather than a generic international product

It is less suitable for:

- Limited companies (FreeAgent supports them but it is designed primarily for sole traders)
- Businesses that need deep third-party integrations or advanced reporting
- Those who work with an accountant who already uses Xero

## Verdict

FreeAgent is the best accounting software for most UK sole traders. The Self Assessment integration is its defining advantage — the entire product is designed around helping one-person businesses understand and manage their UK tax obligations without needing an accountant. The MTD compliance tools, tax timeline, and bank feeds are all well-executed.

At £22.80/month it is a considered purchase, but for anyone filing their own Self Assessment and managing their own bookkeeping, it typically pays for itself. For NatWest, RBS, Ulster Bank, and Mettle customers, there is no decision to make — it is free, and it is genuinely good software.
