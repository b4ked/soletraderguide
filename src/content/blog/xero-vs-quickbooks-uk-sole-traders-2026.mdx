Xero and QuickBooks are the two most widely used paid MTD accounting tools for UK sole traders. Both are HMRC-recognised. Both offer bank feeds, invoicing, and quarterly MTD submissions. Both have 30-day free trials.

The differences matter — but they point to different types of users, not a universal winner. This comparison gives you a clear view of where each one leads.

## Price

| | Xero Ignite | QuickBooks Sole Trader |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly price (ex VAT) | £16/month | £10/month |
| Annual cost (ex VAT) | £192/year | £120/year |
| Free trial | 30 days | 30 days |
| Free plan | No | No |

**QuickBooks wins on price.** At £10/month, it costs £72/year less than Xero Ignite. For a sole trader on a tight budget, that is meaningful. Both prices exclude VAT — add 20% for the actual cost.

The price gap narrows if Xero is running an introductory promotion (common at 50–75% off for the first three to six months). Check both providers' current pricing before committing.

## Where Xero leads

**Accountant adoption.** This is Xero's most significant advantage for UK users. Xero has the widest adoption among UK accountants and bookkeepers. If you involve an accountant at any point — year-end review, occasional advice, or a full-service relationship — they are more likely to already use Xero than any alternative. This means seamless data sharing and no duplicate entry.

**Integrations.** Xero's app marketplace has over 800 third-party integrations: payment processors, CRM tools, project management, inventory, e-commerce platforms. QuickBooks has integrations too, but Xero's ecosystem is broader. For sole traders whose business connects to other software, this matters.

**Landlord support.** Xero explicitly supports UK property income under MTD — useful if you have rental income alongside self-employment. QuickBooks' Sole Trader plan does not support property income in the same way. See our [Xero for landlords guide](/blog/xero-for-landlords-mtd-2026-uk).

**Scalability.** If your business grows — you take on employees, need multi-currency, or transition to a limited company — Xero's higher-tier plans scale well. The learning curve does not reset when you upgrade.

## Where QuickBooks leads

**Price.** £10/month vs £16/month is a clear QuickBooks advantage for budget-conscious sole traders.

**Mobile app and mileage.** QuickBooks has the best mobile app of any mainstream MTD software. Mileage logging is automatic via GPS, expense capture is fast, and the overall mobile experience is more polished. For sole traders who work on the move — tradespeople, delivery workers, field consultants — QuickBooks' mobile functionality is noticeably better.

**Simplicity for self-managed users.** QuickBooks Sole Trader is designed specifically for self-employed individuals who manage their own accounts. The interface is clean and the workflow is focused. If you self-file and do not need accountant collaboration, QuickBooks is straightforward to use.

## Who should choose Xero

- You work with an accountant or plan to
- Your business involves rental income as well as self-employment
- You connect to other software (payment processors, project tools, e-commerce)
- You expect your business to grow beyond sole trader status
- You want the market-leading platform with the broadest professional support

## Who should choose QuickBooks

- You self-manage your own accounts without an accountant
- Price is the primary factor — £10/month is meaningful
- You work on the move and need strong mobile expense capture
- You track significant mileage
- You want a simple, focused sole trader experience
